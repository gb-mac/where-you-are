# Vehicle Storage — Out of Area Operations

---

## The Problem

Home base has the stockpile, the assistant, the infrastructure. When the player is operating far from their GPS home location, none of that is reachable. Beyond roughly 50km, the player needs a node — somewhere to resupply, recover, and regroup that isn't a four-hour drive home hurt.

The Common is that node.

---

## The Common — Introduction

The player doesn't get a tutorial. They get a coin.

Found on a mission — in a pocket, in a dead drop, on a body. Gold. Warden stamp. The player keeps it without knowing what it's for. It feels like it means something.

They go down near a Common. They wake up in a room with a doctor. The doctor explains where they are, who runs the network, what the coin is, and what it got them. That's the introduction — not a waypoint marker, not a UI prompt. You earned your way inside while unconscious and someone decided you were worth pulling through the door.

The coin is Warden currency. Faction-neutral. Valid across the entire Common network. The player who finds one early and holds onto it has infrastructure they don't know they have yet.

**The player who sold it** wakes up in the same room, same doctor. But the Keeper is standing there when they come around. They pulled you in without the coin — someone saw you go down near the door and the Keeper made a call. You're alive. You don't have what you owe. The Keeper will tell you what the debt is when you're ready to hear it.

The debt is not punitive. It's the network operating correctly. The coin is one way in — payment in advance, no questions, no obligation. The debt is another way in — the Keeper extended credit, and credit has terms. Both are relationships with The Common. One you came to prepared. One you came to on the floor.

The debt becomes a questline hook. What the Keeper asks for is specific, proportionate, and tells the player something about how the network actually works. It is not a fine. It is a favour — the kind that builds standing when it's paid.

## The Common as Forward Base

The Common network is distributed. Burke's network has nodes positioned across the region — real GPS-anchored locations: hotels, roadhouses, community halls brought into the network and set up with a Keeper. Not evenly distributed. There are gaps. Some areas have nothing nearby and the player has to know that before they go in.

**What a Common provides at range:**
- Medical care — the Keeper has supplies, may have a medic on rotation
- Resupply — what the network can source, more than a looted pharmacy
- Storage — secure, faction-neutral, accessible when you return
- A bed — real recovery, not field rest
- Information — the Keeper knows the local situation. Who's moving where, what changed since the player was last through, what the other guests have said

**What it costs:** Warden coin. Relationship. Standing. A player who is excommunicado finds the door closed or the welcome conditional. A player with deep Warden standing finds the Keeper already knows them by reputation.

## Getting There While Hurt

The journey to the Common is the mechanic. Not a loading screen — a gauntlet.

The player is hurt, reduced, operating at degraded capacity in territory they may not know well. The Common is 8km away through contested ground. Move carefully, use cover, hide and wait for a patrol to pass, crawl the last stretch if necessary. Moe runs ahead and comes back. The assistant tracks the route if he can.

Arriving is the payoff. The Keeper sees what state the player is in and doesn't comment on it — just gets them inside and gets them what they need. The room, the supplies, the information. The world outside stops for a moment.

**The network has gaps.** Some operations take the player into areas where the nearest Common is 40km out. Burke knows the network better than anyone — if the player has the relationship and the communication, he can route them to the nearest node or to a contact who can shelter them in the gap.

**Unlocking nodes.** New Commons become accessible as the player's network expands — Burke introduces new Keepers, faction relationships open doors, the player completes a questline in a new area and the local Common comes online. The network grows with the player's reach.

## The Vehicle as Mobile Base

When out of area, the vehicle is the player's base. Storage space is limited and finite — what gets loaded before leaving is what's available in the field. Medical supplies, gear, tools, food, fuel. The preparation decision is made before departure, not in the moment of need.

Running low on a supply in the field is a real problem. Restocking options at range:
- **The Common** — resupply, gear maintenance, medical, a place to sleep. The Keeper knows what's needed and can source it or route to someone who can.
- **Faction contacts** — relationships built earlier become resupply nodes. A Reckoner community you helped two months ago has a workshop. A Warden corridor has a House Seat two hours out.
- **Scavenge** — whatever's in the area. Pharmacies, hardware stores, abandoned vehicles. GPS-anchored real locations, assessed at range without Moe's local knowledge of home territory.

---

## Moe Routing

Moe's routing intelligence accounts for the vehicle. If the player goes down at range and the vehicle is the closest resource, Moe leads there first. The vehicle's medical supplies are the tier-2 recovery option before anything else — if they're stocked.

A player who loaded the vehicle well before leaving has options in the field. A player who didn't has Moe looking for the nearest pharmacy in unfamiliar territory.

---

## Vehicle Storage as Preparation Design

The storage constraint creates a genuine pre-mission decision: what does this operation require, and what am I willing to leave behind to fit it?

A long-range operation needs more medical supplies and fuel. A quick operation near a Common needs less. The player who reads the operation correctly before departure is better prepared than the player who loads by habit.

**What the assistant contributes:** Before departure, he can brief the load — *"you're going three hours out, nearest Common is in Reckoner territory, last time you were in that area you needed a specific supply you didn't have."* He's been tracking the patterns. The briefing is actionable, not decorative.

**Vehicle damage and loss.** A vehicle that gets hit loses storage integrity. Supplies can be destroyed or inaccessible. A vehicle that gets taken — by the Hollow, by the Compact, by bad luck — takes the supplies with it. Recoverable, but a real setback at range.
