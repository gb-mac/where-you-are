# Vehicle Storage — Out of Area Operations

---

## The Problem

Home base has the stockpile, the assistant, the infrastructure. When the player is operating far from their GPS home location, none of that is reachable. The vehicle is what they have.

---

## The Vehicle as Mobile Base

When out of area, the vehicle is the player's base. Storage space is limited and finite — what gets loaded before leaving is what's available in the field. Medical supplies, gear, tools, food, fuel. The preparation decision is made before departure, not in the moment of need.

Running low on a supply in the field is a real problem. Restocking options at range:
- **Wayhouses** — the Continental network exists specifically for this. Resupply, gear maintenance, medical, a place to sleep. The Keeper knows what's needed and can source it or route to someone who can. The Wayhouse network is infrastructure for exactly the situation of being far from home.
- **Faction contacts** — relationships built earlier become resupply nodes. A Reckoner community you helped two months ago has a workshop. A Warden corridor has a House Seat two hours out.
- **Scavenge** — whatever's in the area. Pharmacies, hardware stores, abandoned vehicles. The same GPS-anchored real locations, now assessed at range without Moe's local knowledge of home territory.

---

## Moe Routing Update

Moe's routing intelligence accounts for the vehicle. If the player goes down at range and the vehicle is the closest resource, Moe leads there first. The vehicle's medical supplies are the tier-2 recovery option before anything else — if they're stocked.

A player who loaded the vehicle well before leaving has options in the field. A player who didn't has Moe looking for the nearest pharmacy in unfamiliar territory.

---

## Vehicle Storage as Preparation Design

The storage constraint creates a genuine pre-mission decision: what does this operation require, and what am I willing to leave behind to fit it?

A long-range operation needs more medical supplies and fuel. A quick operation near a Wayhouse node needs less. The player who reads the operation correctly before departure is better prepared than the player who loads by habit.

**What the assistant contributes:** Before departure, he can brief the load — *"you're going three hours out, nearest Wayhouse is in Reckoner territory, last time you were in that area you needed a specific supply you didn't have."* He's been tracking the patterns. The briefing is actionable, not decorative.

**Vehicle damage and loss.** A vehicle that gets hit loses storage integrity. Supplies can be destroyed or inaccessible. A vehicle that gets taken — by the Hollow, by the Compact, by bad luck — takes the supplies with it. This is recoverable but it's a real setback at range, which is exactly when you can least afford it.
