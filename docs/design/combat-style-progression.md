# Combat Style Progression

*"It's not like John Wick used the same moves all through the same movie, let alone all 4."*

---

## The Problem It Solves

A player two years into WYA should not look like a Day 1 player. Not just because they have better gear — because they move differently. The close combat system needs to evolve: styles layer, replace, and cycle as the player's history accumulates.

Static movesets are a design failure. They tell the player nothing has changed. The combat system should be a visible record of what the player has been through.

---

## The John Wick Model

Wick works because each movie he's operating under different constraints — different environment, different resources, different physical state, different opponent caliber. The Judo gives way to Jiu-Jitsu gives way to tactical improvisation. The style shifts because the situation demands it. He doesn't forget what he knew. He adapts on top of it.

For WYA: the player doesn't unlock new moves and forget old ones. They develop a vocabulary that deepens. Early moves become the foundation. Later styles layer over them. The combination is what makes a veteran player readable — you can see the history in how they fight.

---

## What Drives Style Evolution

**Faction alignment** — The player's faction affiliation shapes their close combat vocabulary over time. Warden-adjacent players develop disciplined, defensive technique — hold the line, protect what matters. Reckoner-adjacent players develop aggressive, economical technique — end it fast, don't waste movement. The Compact trains differently than the Hollow fights. The system reads where the player has been spending their time.

**Engagement history** — What the player has actually faced shapes what they've learned. Repeated close-quarter ambushes teach a different skill set than open-ground encounters. The combat system tracks this. The style shifts to reflect it.

**Real-world time** — A player two years in has had two years. The system treats this as legitimate progression input, not just session count.

**Physical state** — Injury affects style. The player coming back from a significant hit moves differently for a session. The assistant may comment on it. The combat system reflects it visually.

---

## Style Cycling

Styles don't accumulate forever — they cycle. A player who has been doing Warden-adjacent work for six months and then spends three months deep in Reckoner operations will see the earlier style recede and the new one come forward. Not erased — available, but not dominant.

This means a player who switches faction alignment or operation type watches their combat identity shift. It's a narrative signal as much as a mechanical one.

---

## Close Combat Focus

The evolution system is primarily for close combat. Ranged and tactical play have their own progression, but the *visible* character of the player's development shows most clearly at close range — footwork, clinch behavior, how they manage distance, what they do when the weapon is gone.

This is where the history lives. This is where two years looks different from two weeks.

---

## The Assistant's Role

He notices. He comments occasionally — not as a coach, as someone who has been watching.

*"You're moving differently than you were six months ago."*

He doesn't elaborate unless asked. He's noting it because he's been paying attention. The player can take it as data or not.

He also delivers gear reactively to complement the player's developing style — not to override it. If the player's close combat has evolved toward grapple-heavy technique, the gear he sources reflects that. He's reading the same history the system is reading.
