# Radio System

## The Rule

The radio is not a feature. It is infrastructure.

Radios broke during the Cascade and never fully recovered. They come on when they come on. They go off when they go off. The player has no control — no on switch, no off switch, no channel selection. It lands on what it lands on.

**This is a design rule, not a default setting. The player never controls the radio.**

---

## What Plays

Multiple broadcast sources exist in the world. The broken radio cycles between them unpredictably:

- **Aldric** — Reckoner broadcast, all-faction news, commentary, the AP wire
- **Maren** — mutual aid network, recipes, gardening tips, community updates, seasonal/regional content
- **Covenant broadcast** — Shepherd-run outreach, calm and certain, the frame that makes everything the Compact and Reckoners do sound like fear
- **Static** — the most common state
- **Fragments** — partial transmissions, things that were broadcasting before the Cascade and never stopped, things that shouldn't still be on air

---

## When It Plays

Ambient — during home base activities (building, gardening, cooking), travel (driving, walking to a mission), and open-world exploration.

It does not play during active combat as a primary mechanic, but it does not respect mission boundaries. The radio is infrastructure. Infrastructure doesn't check whether you're ready.

---

## The Liability

The radio comes on during a mission. The player is hiding. A Compact patrol is close. A passionate Covenant broadcast opens up from a nearby speaker or vehicle radio. The player can't turn it off. Their position is potentially compromised. They have to move or adapt.

The radio doesn't know. It just comes on. The world doesn't care about timing.

This is intentional. The radio being uncontrollable means it is occasionally a genuine problem. That's what makes it real — it has consequences, not just atmosphere.

---

## Narrative Function

The player cannot curate their information environment. They hear what the world is broadcasting, the same as everyone else. Covenant propaganda arrives the same way the news does. The broken radio is the delivery mechanism nobody opted into.

Over two years of play, the broadcast layer accumulates. Fragments that didn't make sense in Act 1 land differently in Act 3. Aldric reported something six months ago that the player half-heard while building. It matters now.

The radio rewards the player who listens. It doesn't punish the player who doesn't — they just carry less context.
