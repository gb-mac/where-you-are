# The Impossible Task — Restoration v0.2
*The Task is the verdict. There is no hearing.*

---

## The Offer

The Table doesn't come to you. They never do.

An intermediary makes contact — a Sworn you've encountered before, or a House servant, someone with just enough standing to deliver a message and just enough deniability that the Wardens can claim no official approach was made. The message is brief. There is no negotiation on the terms.

*"The Table has discussed your Record. There is something that needs doing — something the Vanguard cannot be seen doing. Complete it. You're back in."*

No hearing. No testimony. No vote. The Table has already deliberated. The task is the verdict — pass it, and Restoration is immediate and unconditional. The intermediary will be watching. When it's done, they'll know.

Fail, and the task remains open — try again, or walk away. The Table will not chase you. They will simply wait.

No backup. No Neutral Ground. No Markers. You are still excommunicado until the moment it's done. The Table is not lifting that to give you an easier path — they are watching how you walk a hard one.

---

## Why the Table Does This

The Impossible Task is not a test. It is a transaction — and the Table is honest about that.

They have a problem they cannot solve through official channels. They have a person outside those channels who has demonstrated enough to be considered. The math is simple. The Wardens benefit from the outcome. The player gets their standing back. No pretense that it's anything more complicated.

What makes it genuinely difficult — and what separates it from a job posting — is this: **the target knows someone is coming.**

Not who. Not exactly when. But the Wardens have allowed enough information to reach the target that they are prepared. A Behemoth agitated by recent Vanguard activity. A Riven leader warned through Hollow channels that their position is compromised. A rogue commander who tightened security after receiving intelligence they shouldn't have had.

The Table chose the target. The Table chose to warn them. The player walks in without support, against a prepared adversary, and either gets it done or doesn't. That is the whole of it.

## Target Types

The specific target depends on the region, the nature of the original violation, and what the Wardens currently need. Four primary target categories:

---

### Type 1 — The Named Behemoth

A Fractured machine that has accumulated long enough to reach Behemoth tier — and has moved into or is threatening territory under Warden stewardship. The Vanguard have attempted to drive it off twice. The second attempt cost them two Vanguard and one Sworn. The Wardens cannot publicly commit more resources to this without demonstrating that they cannot protect their own territory, which has political consequences they cannot afford.

The player is given:
- The Behemoth's last known location and approximate territory
- A brief record of the Vanguard attempts — what they tried, what failed
- Nothing else

The Behemoth is Composite or Behemoth tier. Its accumulation reflects the location type — a rural Behemoth has incorporated agricultural machinery at a scale that makes Vanguard engagements catastrophic. An industrial Behemoth has crane components and hydraulic systems that the prior attempts underestimated.

**What "the target knows someone is coming" means for a Behemoth:**
The Vanguard's previous attempts have made it more reactive — it has learned, in the way Fractured machines learn, that the humans near this territory are a threat. Its territorial behavior has expanded. It moves to the edges of its zone and watches. It responds to approach faster than a fresh Behemoth would.

The player cannot simply sneak up on it. They have to understand its patterns well enough to find an approach that the prior two Vanguard attempts didn't use.

**Reward beyond the hearing:** A Behemoth that falls releases its accumulated components — the most concentrated cache of machine parts available anywhere in the region. This is also available to any players who assist (the task doesn't prohibit help from non-Warden players — only Warden-aligned players cannot officially assist).

---

### Type 2 — The Riven Leader

A named leader of a Riven Hollow group — the Architect tier, someone who has built genuine devotion around a predatory philosophy and is actively expanding into Warden-protected territory. The Vanguard's code prevents them from targeting an individual preemptively; they can only respond to acts of aggression, and the Riven leader has been careful to act through subordinates. Every action that could be formally attributed to them involves one degree of separation.

The Wardens cannot move against this person under the code. The player is not bound by the code right now.

The player is given:
- The Riven leader's known base of operations
- A profile built from Arbiter observations and Signal data — personality, habits, what they value, how they've operated
- The names of three lieutenants who serve as the degrees of separation
- Nothing else

**What "the target knows someone is coming" means for a Riven leader:**
They were warned — vaguely, through Hollow intelligence networks — that someone outside normal faction channels has been asking about them. They don't know it's an excommunicado player on a Warden task. They know it's someone who isn't operating under usual constraints. They've tightened their inner circle and moved their location twice in the last week.

The player has to locate them, then reach them through or around the lieutenants, then make a decision about what "take out" actually means. The Wardens said complete the task. They didn't specify the method. A Riven leader who is dead is one solution. A Riven leader who is publicly, irreversibly discredited — their theology exposed as performance, their inner circle turned against them, their community collapsed from within — is another. The Table will accept either. The Record will note which one the player chose.

---

### Type 3 — The Rogue Commander

An Iron Compact commander who has gone off-mandate. They are running Project Tether operations — capturing and attempting to reprogram Awakened machines — on territory adjacent to or within Warden stewardship zones. They are doing this without Compact authorization (or with authorization that has been quietly revoked and they haven't acknowledged). The machines they have broken have become Corrupted, and several of those Corrupted machines have wandered into Warden-protected communities.

The Compact cannot officially move against one of their own commanders without a tribunal that would expose Project Tether publicly. The Wardens cannot confront a Compact commander directly without triggering the friction between the two factions into open conflict.

The player is given:
- The commander's installation location
- A partial record of Project Tether operations compiled by the Signal, shared through the Arbiter/Signal relationship
- The names of the officers under the commander who are following orders versus those who have reservations
- Nothing else

**What "the target knows someone is coming" means for a rogue commander:**
They have military intelligence resources. Someone — probably a Compact Hardliner who wants the excommunicado player neutralized — tipped them that an outside actor has been making inquiries about the installation. The commander has increased perimeter security and moved the most sensitive Project Tether subjects deeper into the installation.

"Take out" here is deliberately ambiguous. The Wardens want the Tether operations stopped and the commander's ability to operate in their territory eliminated. That could mean: the commander's death, their arrest and formal Compact tribunal (which the player would have to engineer), their exposure to the Signal with enough evidence that publication makes their position untenable, or the destruction of the installation itself. Each approach has different consequences for the Compact's regional political balance and the Warden/Compact relationship.

The player will have to navigate Compact soldiers who are just doing their jobs, officers who might be turned, and a commander who is genuinely convinced they are right.

---

### Type 4 — The Syndicate Architect

A Hollow Syndicate boss who has built a supply network predicated on raiding Warden corridor routes — not the estates themselves (the code protects those), but the roads and paths between them that Warden-allied communities depend on. Supplies are being taxed. Travelers are being taken. The Syndicate is careful enough to avoid the technical definitions of Neutral Ground violation, which means the Wardens cannot respond through formal channels.

The player is given:
- The Syndicate's operational territory and known safe houses
- The network structure — who reports to whom, where the supply chain runs, what leverage points exist
- One contact inside the Syndicate who has private reasons to see the current leadership change — not a Warden asset, just someone the Wardens know exists and have chosen not to mention to the Syndicate
- Nothing else

**What "the target knows someone is coming" means for a Syndicate boss:**
They run an intelligence operation. They know an excommunicado player has been operating in their territory. They don't know the Wardens are involved — officially, the Wardens aren't. They've put a bounty on the player through Hollow channels, independent of the Hunt, because having an unaffiliated wild card in their operational zone is bad for business.

The player is simultaneously hunted by the Syndicate and tasked with dismantling it. The inside contact is real — but whether to trust them, use them, or burn them is the player's call. The Syndicate boss may also be negotiated with: there is a version of this where the boss agrees to leave the Warden corridors alone in exchange for something. The Wardens' task said "take out the threat to our routes." It didn't specify the boss personally has to go.

## Completion

When the target is down — by whatever method the player chose — the intermediary reappears. They don't ask how. They don't ask what it cost. The message is four words:

*"Welcome back. You're Sworn."*

The Mark changes in the AR overlay — immediately, visibly, for every player with Warden standing in the region. The excommunicado entry remains in the Record. Alongside it, a new entry:

*"Restoration granted. Task completed. [Target name]. [Method]. — Table, witnessed by [Intermediary name]."*

No ceremony. No estate. No handshake. The Restoration happens in the field, at the location where the task was completed, between the player and one Sworn who was sent to watch.

The player walks back into the world with their standing restored. The Record shows everything — what they did, what they were, what they did to come back. Every faction that received the excommunicado notification receives the Restoration notification. The Hollow who had a bounty on them get a quiet message that the contract has been cancelled. The Iron Compact notes it and files it.

It's done.

---

## If the Player Refuses or Abandons

The task remains open indefinitely. The Table does not rescind the offer — they simply don't schedule the hearing. The world continues. The excommunicado status continues.

If the player returns to the intermediary weeks or months later and asks if the offer still stands: *"The task is what it is. It doesn't change."*

Some players will never take the task. That is a legitimate path. The excommunicado arc doesn't require Restoration. It requires only that the player decide what they're going to be.

## Narrative Note — What This Is

No hearing. No testimony. No staged journey through faction questlines building a case.

The Table named a target. You took it out. You're back in.

That's the whole thing. The code is formal and the Wardens believe in it — but they're also practical people who have been running territory through a collapse, and they know what a capable person looks like, and they know what it costs to waste one.

The task is not contempt. It's not a punishment designed to be survived. It's the Table saying: *we have a problem, you have something to prove, let's see if the math works.*

It worked. Four words and a Record entry.

Welcome back.
