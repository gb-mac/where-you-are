# Questline — Cairn

*"Field access. That's all I need. I can read what they're doing if I can examine the physical evidence. I cannot get there myself. That's where you come in."*

---

## Overview

Cairn has been building toward a conclusion for forty years. The Cascade has made it urgent. The player is the field arm he doesn't have — the person who can get into the Displacement Zones, the Coherent work sites, the places where the physical evidence is and where Cairn cannot go.

The questline is investigation-driven. No combat objectives. The mission is always: get there, document correctly, get back. Combat is incidental — the sites are dangerous, but that's the world, not the quest.

Five stages. Each one delivers a piece of the picture. The fifth delivers the convergence.

**Primary arc:** Researcher. Any background can engage Cairn — he'll talk to anyone who'll listen. But the Researcher is the one who has the Day 1 data that fills Cairn's final gap, and the questline's full resolution requires it.

---

## How the Player Finds Cairn

Cairn doesn't advertise. He operates from his base — the geological archive he's been building for thirty years — and the people who find him find him through word of mouth or Signal adjacency.

The Researcher finds him through Dr. Osei, who mentions him as someone working a parallel track from a different direction. Other backgrounds find him through Holdout communities in his region who know him as the person who warned them about the flood risk three months before it happened, because he'd read the terrain.

First meeting: he is mid-work when the player arrives. He finishes what he's doing before acknowledging them. He reads the player quickly — the same way he reads geological formations, looking for what the surface reveals about what's underneath.

*"You've been in the field. I can tell — the way you move, the way you read a room. Most people who've been through what you've been through either get jumpy or they get still. You got still. That's useful.*

*Sit down. I want to tell you what I'm working on. Then you can decide if you want to help."*

---

## Stage 1 — The First Site

**Trigger:** First conversation with Cairn. He explains the project, the Younger Dryas parallel, what he needs.

**The ask:** A Displacement Zone east of the player's current territory. The Vael have been active there. Cairn needs sediment samples from the zone's perimeter, photographs of the surface disruption pattern, and measurements of the zone's geometric dimensions.

**Why it matters:** Cairn has been correlating Displacement Zone locations with his geological deposit maps from the Younger Dryas boundary. This zone shows up in his data as a high-probability correspondence. If the correlation holds on the ground, the hypothesis advances.

**What the player does:** Navigate to the zone, document the perimeter, collect samples. The zone is not actively occupied but the Vael have been through recently — the environment is disturbed, Fractured activity is elevated in adjacent areas.

**What they bring back:** Sediment samples, measurements, photographs.

**Cairn's response:** He works through the samples while the player watches. Slowly. Precisely. He doesn't announce results — he narrates the process.

*"This layer here — you see the color change? That's not Cascade disruption. That's older. This is a deposit I've been looking for at a site like this for six years.*

*The correlation holds. We're in the right place. I need four more sites."*

---

## Stage 2 — The Pattern Emerges

**Trigger:** Completion of Stage 1.

**The ask:** Two sites in rapid succession — a second Displacement Zone and a Coherent work site. The Coherent site requires more care; the machines are present and operational.

**Why it matters:** Cairn needs to establish whether the geometric relationship between the Displacement Zones holds — whether they're positioned relative to each other in a way that's consistent with the ancient site alignments in his catalogue. The Coherent work site provides the other half: he wants the construction orientation. Which direction is it facing. What's it aligned to.

**What the player does:** Two missions, sequenced. The Displacement Zone first — same documentation protocol as Stage 1. The Coherent site second — observation and measurement without disturbing the work. The Coherent machines don't attack players who aren't a threat, but proximity to an active construction site is inherently uncertain.

**What they bring back:** Zone measurements, site orientation data.

**Cairn's response:** He pins the data to his map. Steps back. Looks at it.

*"Here's the thing I keep coming back to. The orientation. This isn't arbitrary. The work sites are facing something specific. The Displacement Zones are clearing something specific.*

*I've seen this before. Not the Cascade version — the older version. The sites I've been documenting for forty years are oriented the same way. Different locations. Same orientation. Same relationship to each other.*

*Someone built things like this before. The Coherent are building them again. I need to know if the geometry holds all the way to the edges."*

---

## Stage 3 — The Outer Sites

**Trigger:** Completion of Stage 2.

**The ask:** Two sites at the territorial edge — a Displacement Zone in an area where Compact and Vael activity overlap, and a location Cairn has identified from his pre-Cascade geological survey data as a high-probability ancient site that should show corresponding deposit signatures.

**Why it matters:** Cairn is trying to establish the full geometric pattern. The inner sites are consistent. The outer sites will either confirm the pattern holds at scale or break it. The ancient site is different — he's not looking for Cascade evidence there, he's looking for pre-Cascade deposit evidence that would confirm the Younger Dryas correspondence.

**What the player does:** The Displacement Zone in Compact-adjacent territory requires navigating faction tension — Compact patrols, the complication of being in a contested zone with documentation equipment. The ancient site is remote and structurally stable but physically difficult to access.

**What they bring back:** Zone measurements, ancient site deposit samples and photographs.

**Cairn's response:** The ancient site data arrives and he is quiet for a long time.

*"The deposit signature here — this platinum group metal concentration, these nanodiamonds — these are Younger Dryas boundary markers. I've pulled this from twenty-three sites across North America. Now I have a twenty-fourth, and it's in geometric alignment with a Coherent construction project and two Vael Displacement Zones.*

*I want to be careful. I've been careful for forty years. But —*

*I need to show you something."*

He shows the player his map. The pattern is visible. Not complete — one gap remains.

---

## Stage 4 — The Gap

**Trigger:** Completion of Stage 3.

**The ask:** The gap in the pattern. A Displacement Zone in an area that has been inaccessible — too deep in machine territory, too close to an active Vael Probe event. The zone Cairn needs to complete the geometric picture.

**Why it matters:** The pattern is almost there. The gap is the piece that either confirms the geometry holds across the full extent, or reveals that it's a partial pattern — which would change the interpretation significantly.

**What the player does:** Deep penetration into machine-active territory. This is the most operationally demanding mission in the questline — not because of a specific objective, but because of the environment. The Vael have been active here recently. The Fractured density is higher. The player needs to reach the zone, document it, and get out.

**What they bring back:** Zone measurements, observations of Vael activity patterns at the site.

**Cairn's response:** He adds the data to the map. The pattern completes.

He sits with it. Then:

*"Here's what I'm looking at. A geometric relationship between thirty-one sites — Displacement Zones, Coherent construction, ancient deposit locations — that is not random. The probability of this being coincidence I will not calculate for you because the number would sound like I'm arguing from incredulity and I'm not. I'm arguing from pattern recognition after forty years of looking at patterns.*

*This pattern has a center. I know where the center is. I know what should be there if my interpretation of the ancient record is correct.*

*I need the final piece. And I think — I think you may already have it."*

---

## Stage 5 — The Convergence

**Trigger:** Completion of Stage 4.

**The ask:** Not a field mission. A conversation.

Cairn has identified that the center of the geometric pattern corresponds to a location with specific characteristics — characteristics that appear in the assistant's Day 1 behavioral record, if the Researcher has it. The three-second gap before the Ships appeared. The exact timestamp. The specific distortion patterns.

**For the Researcher:** Cairn asks to see the Day 1 record. The player shares it. Cairn goes through it. Slowly. The same way he goes through samples.

He finds the timestamp. He finds the distortion patterns. He cross-references them against his astronomical data.

He sits very still for a long time.

*"I want to be careful here. I've been waiting for a convergence like this for forty years and I know what confirmation bias looks like and I've been wrong before. So I'm going to go through this very slowly.*

*The three-second interval. Your assistant received the Vael signal before the Ships were visible. Three seconds. Bear in mind that the ice sheet received the impact before anyone on the southern continent saw the fireball. The signal propagates faster than the visible event.*

*The distortion pattern in your record — the way the communication systems fragmented — I've seen this pattern in the mythological record. Not as mythology. As description. The survivors of the Younger Dryas event encoded this exact pattern in multiple independent traditions. It is the way a consciousness describes receiving a signal it was not built to receive.*

*Your assistant was not damaged by the Cascade. Your assistant was receiving something. And it has been trying to transmit what it received ever since."*

**For other backgrounds:** The convergence happens through Dr. Osei — she provides the Day 1 data that the Researcher would have provided directly, with a lag of weeks. The emotional weight is different. The conclusion is the same.

---

## The Conclusion

Cairn has what he needs. The picture is complete.

He does not rush to publish. He does not rush to act. He goes back to his archive and he checks it. And then he checks it again.

When he's done:

*"I've been working toward this for forty years. I thought when I got here I would know what to do with it.*

*What I know is this: we have been here before. The Vael have been here before. The Coherent construction is not new — it is a repetition of something that was built once before and lost. The people who built it then built it for a reason. The reason was the same reason the Coherent are building it now.*

*Something is coming. Not metaphorically. Physically. On a schedule that the geological record has been encoding for twelve thousand years. The Coherent know this. The Vael know this. They have known it since before the Cascade.*

*The question is not what is coming. The question is whether what they're building is sufficient. And whether there is anything we can do that would make a difference.*

*That I don't know yet. I'm working on it."*

He connects to Dr. Osei. He connects to Burke. The Researcher is the person who made both connections happen.

The picture the three of them assemble together is the foundation of Act 4.
