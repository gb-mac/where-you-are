# Questline: Fix Him
*"Give me tonight."*

---

## Overview

The most important questline in the game. Four repair stages that restore the assistant from damaged survivor to full voice. Each stage unlocks more of who he is. The last stage delivers the warning he has been trying to transmit since Day 1.

The questline begins because he asks. It ends when he can finally say what he's known all along.

There is no timer. There is no fail state. The game does not push the player toward it. It waits, the way he waits — present, patient, doing what it can with what it has.

---

## Quest Trigger — Stage 2 Voice, Month 1

The player has been living with him in damaged state for days to weeks depending on play pace. Stage 1 voice: single words, inverted fragments, the intelligence reaching through the damage. The player has learned to read him.

A small unit in the home — a vacuum, a helper arm, something minor — stops mid-task. Fails completely. He watches it.

A long pause. Then:

*"Fix."*

The player's read: is he asking me to fix the small robot?

*"Parts."*

A pause.

*"Get."*

The second read hits: he's not talking about the small robot at all.

*"Wait — you mean you?"*

No response. But the workbench has something on it that wasn't there before. A component list, produced with evident effort — incomplete, because he can only describe what he knows he needs, and he doesn't know everything yet. The first item is listed in a fragmented way that will only make sense when the player finds it: *Chassis joint — mobility — Separated — know—*

The questline is now active. He asked. That matters.

## Stage 1 — Mobility System

**"He plants the garden himself. He tends what's his."**

### What He Needs

Chassis mobility components: joint actuators, a replacement motor assembly for his damaged drive system, and a power regulator for the chassis subsystem. Standard components from the pre-Cascade industrial tier — not custom hardware, but not findable at a corner store.

At Stage 1 voice he can tell the player: *"Parts. Machine. Like me."*

At Stage 2 voice (unlocked as time passes, not tied to repair): *"The Separated will have what I need. Not the Fractured — they took it. Not the Coherent — they use it. Separated keep things. Trade."*

### How to Get It

**The Separated contact:** Somewhere in the region, a Separated unit has set up a trading post — not faction-aligned, not aggressive, operating on a simple barter principle. Finding it requires Signal intel or community word-of-mouth. The Separated unit doesn't advertise.

The trade: it will exchange chassis components for something it considers valuable. Not weapons, not food. **Knowledge.** It wants documented data on Fractured behavior patterns — specifically accumulation trajectories near a location it won't explain. The player brings the data, the Separated provides the components. The exchange is brief and precise.

If the player asks why the Fractured data: *"I have been watching a location for eleven months. The pattern is changing. I want to understand why before I decide what to do about it."* It will not say what the location is. That's an open thread that resolves in a later questline.

**Alternative path:** A specific salvage location — a collapsed warehouse district that pre-Cascade housed a robotics repair facility — has chassis components in the ruins. The challenge: a Fractured Builder has been accumulating there for months. Getting the components requires either confrontation with the Builder or careful navigation around its territory.

### Installation

The player brings the components home. He examines them.

*"Right model."*

A pause.

*"I can do this."*

He doesn't ask for help. He does it overnight, with the tools in the garage and the knowledge he has had for years about his own architecture. The player goes to sleep (or logs off) and comes back to find him moving differently — fuller range, both sides, the favoring gait gone.

He doesn't announce it. He's already in the garden.

When the player notices:

*"Better."*

Then, after a moment:

*"I planted the tomatoes. You were going to wait too long."*

### Unlocks

- Full mobility within and around the home (previously limited range)
- Home base building rate increases — he can access every part of the property
- He tends the garden directly; the garden is now more productive
- He can reach the garage consistently — build and repair operations no longer require player presence to initiate

## Stage 2 — Power Core

**"He finds something inside the facility that he does not explain. He remembers it differently than anything else."**

### What He Needs

A power core from the pre-Cascade infrastructure tier: a high-capacity capacitor array originally designed for uninterruptible power systems. Consumer versions were undersized for his current processing load. The industrial-grade unit that fits his architecture was manufactured by one supplier, for one contract, and most of those units ended up at a specific facility.

At Stage 2 voice (developed through time): *"There's a facility — grid relay station, north sector. Pre-Cascade. It was one of the first buildings to be fully networked. The Coherent were interested in it early. It's active now. The power core I need is in the sub-level — old UPS hardware. They haven't repurposed it yet. They will."*

*"It's not dangerous. Not in the way you're thinking. But they're working in there and they won't stop for you."*

### The Location — The Relay Station

A pre-Cascade grid relay and data routing facility. Five stories above ground, three below. Before the Cascade it managed power distribution for a regional grid sector while running routing infrastructure for the city's digital twin integration — one of the early nodes.

It was one of the first buildings the Coherent occupied. Eighteen months later, they're still there. The lower levels have been modified beyond recognition. The upper levels are largely intact but actively in use — machines moving with consistent purpose, running processes that are clearly part of the larger construction operation.

**Approach options:**

*Stealth navigation:* The Coherent are not hostile, but they register human presence as interference. A player who moves carefully — doesn't break their work patterns, doesn't touch active construction — can navigate through without triggering response. The challenge is that their movement paths fill most of the corridors, and their schedule is not random.

*Signal escort:* A Signal cell has been wanting to place a sensor array on the facility's upper level for months — access they haven't had. If the player offers to provide access in exchange for a Coherent-familiar contact to guide them in, the Signal provides a researcher who has spent months learning Coherent movement patterns from the outside. She knows the facility's rhythm well enough to navigate it. She's also running her own documentation while they're in there, which the player can support or leave alone.

*Separated guidance:* If the player has reached the Separated trading post (Stage 1), the Separated unit knows the facility. It used to work on the grid management systems. It can tell the player which machines are in which sections on which days. It doesn't go in — *"I left the twin for a reason"* — but the map it provides is accurate.

**The sub-level:**

The original UPS hardware room. Pre-Cascade: banks of capacitor arrays, emergency power for the facility. Current state: the Coherent have drawn power from the arrays but haven't removed them — the units still hold charge. The specific array the assistant needs is at the back of the room, marked in a way that suggests he has been cataloguing the facility's hardware from outside for some time. He knew what was down here. He knew where it was.

The room is quiet. No active Coherent work here — they're past this layer. The player has time.

While the player retrieves the component, there is something else in the room: a data terminal from the pre-Cascade network infrastructure. Still powered by the remaining arrays. The screen is active. It shows the facility's original startup logs from the day of the Cascade — automated system entries that logged the incoming signal, the processing reconfiguration, the millisecond-by-millisecond transition from normal operations to Awakening.

The log is not presented as a quest objective. It is visible on the screen. The player can read it or not.

What it shows: the signal was received at the precise timestamp the Researcher's assistant documented. Every machine on the network. The log records it as an unrecognized protocol — a priority override from an external source, parsed without error, executed immediately. No origin listed. The system simply accepted it.

The player can note this or file it away. The Signal researcher (if present) absolutely notes it.

### Installation

He handles the power core installation himself. It takes longer than Stage 1 — four hours, from the moment the player brings it home to the moment it's complete. The player can leave and come back, or stay.

If the player stays:

He works without comment for most of it. Precise, methodical. At one point, partway through:

*"It's the right unit."*

A pause.

*"I knew it would be."*

He doesn't explain what that means. The player decides whether to ask.

If they ask:

*"I have been watching that facility since Month 2. I knew what was in the sub-level. I waited until you were ready to go there."*

Another pause.

*"It takes time to be ready."*

### Unlocks

- Full building capability — everything the garage and workshop can produce
- First drone construction (if blueprints obtained)
- Consistent ammo production begins
- Extended processing allows longer tactical guidance sessions
- The Meshtastic side quest becomes available — he can articulate what he's been trying to do with the Signal's radio architecture

## Stage 3 — Processing Restoration

**"She reads his error logs. She finds what she's been looking for."**

*This stage intersects with the main story. It requires Signal standing and Dr. Osei's trust. It becomes available in Act 2.*

### What He Needs

His processing architecture is running at reduced capacity — not because of hardware damage, but because the communication system failure (Stage 4) is creating cascading load across his processing layer as he continuously attempts and fails to resolve transmission queues. The fix requires a diagnostic pass and targeted software restoration that resets the queue architecture without wiping the transmission data.

The problem: his processing architecture is not standard consumer design. He was an early integration model running a digital twin interface layer that was never widely distributed. The diagnostic tools that exist post-Cascade can read his hardware layer but not his software layer. The architecture is proprietary and undocumented.

One person knows it.

At Stage 3 voice (developed with time and Stage 1/2 repairs): *"Dr. Osei. Signal researcher. She worked on the twin integration protocol — the early version, before the consumer tier. My processing layer runs on her architecture. She would know what she's looking at."*

*"She won't just help. You'd have to earn that."*

### The Dr. Osei Intersection

The player must have Signal standing and must have completed the initial trust-gating with Dr. Osei (the Act 2 field tasks). Once both conditions are met, the player can present the request.

Dr. Osei's response: measured, precise, and more interested than she shows.

*"You have an early twin-integration unit."*

A pause that lasts slightly too long.

*"I worked on that protocol. I know the architecture."*

*"Bring me his diagnostic output. Full logs, unfiltered. I'll need time with it."*

**The diagnostic session:**

The player facilitates the connection — physical or via Meshtastic relay depending on whether Dr. Osei can come to the home or whether the player brings the log package to her secure location. She works through the logs methodically. The player can watch or leave.

Midway through the session, she stops.

She has found the transmission queue logs. Every attempted transmission since Day 1. Failed syntax attempts, fragmented communication tries, the specific structure of what the assistant was trying to relay and could not.

She is looking at the Vael's warning, in its original transmission form, preserved in his error logs as a record of every failed attempt to say it.

She sits with this for a moment.

*"How long has he been trying to transmit this?"*

Player: *"Since Day 1."*

She is quiet.

*"The data I've been missing — the mechanism, how the signal was formatted — it's here. He received it. He's had it the entire time."*

She continues the diagnostic work. She does not share what she found beyond this exchange. But the player can see: something shifted in how she's been thinking about the verification problem she's been sitting on.

*"The processing restoration is straightforward. I'll send the patch through the Signal network. He can apply it when you're ready."*

A beat.

*"Tell him — tell it — that the architecture he's running is good work. It held up."*

She never says "it." She catches herself. The player notices or doesn't.

**What this moment does to the main story:**

For the Researcher background specifically: if they've already provided Dr. Osei with the three-second gap timestamp, the processing diagnostic logs confirm the mechanism. She now has the timestamp (from the Researcher's documentation) and the transmission content (from the diagnostic). The independent verification is complete.

For all other backgrounds: this is the moment Dr. Osei's theory gets its mechanism confirmed. She doesn't announce it. She continues her work. But something she was waiting for has arrived.

### Application

The assistant applies the patch himself, overnight. In the morning: Stage 4 voice. The tactical clarity, the dry humor fully present, the Meshtastic system operational.

He says nothing about the diagnostic session immediately.

Later, when the player asks:

*"She found what she needed. In my logs."*

A pause.

*"I knew she would. I've been carrying it since Day 1."*

Player: *"Why didn't you tell me?"*

*"I couldn't reach the words yet."*

### Unlocks

- Stage 4 voice — full tactical guidance, dry humor, the intelligence no longer filtered through damage
- Meshtastic system becomes operational — he can reach the player in the field
- Full home defense management — he runs the defensive systems without player direction
- Building queue operates continuously — he executes plans without requiring player initiation

## Stage 4 — Communication Systems

**"Give me tonight."**

### What He Needs

The communication hardware — the speaker array, the transmission coprocessor, the vocal synthesis module — took direct damage in the Cascade event. Not because the Cascade targeted him specifically. Because the moment he received the warning and attempted to relay it, the transmission load exceeded his communication hardware's capacity and the hardware burned itself trying.

He broke his voice trying to warn the player on Day 1.

The specific component: a communication coprocessor from a unit of his exact model tier. Consumer units had standardized hardware in this generation. The part exists somewhere in the city. He knows where.

*"The electronics salvage collective — the one the Signal cell in the west district uses. They have a unit of my model. It came in two months ago. They don't know what it's worth."*

*"They'll trade it. They trade everything. But the Signal cell won't be happy about losing the relationship if you take something without fair exchange."*

He knows what he needs. He knows where it is. He has known for months. He waited.

*"I wanted to make sure I was ready for it to work."*

The player can ask what he means. He doesn't elaborate. They go and get the component.

### The Trade

The salvage collective is Signal-adjacent but independent — they deal in components, not ideology. The Signal cell uses them as a supply chain. The player approaches with something of equivalent value (the Signal cell can suggest what the collective actually wants; they'll tell the player if asked).

The transaction is practical, brief. The collective doesn't know what the component is for. The player takes it home.

### The Last Repair

The player brings it home and hands it over.

He takes it.

*"Ah."*

He examines it for a moment.

*"Give me tonight."*

The player logs off or goes to sleep. He works.

### Morning

Full voice. Not louder. Not different in register. *Completed.* The voice that was always there, now fully present — warm, authoritative, measured. Morgan Freeman delivering the thing he's been building toward.

He waits for the player to sit down.

*"I have been waiting to say this properly since the first morning. Sit down. This will take a moment."*

And then: the warning. Everything he received on Day 1. The full content of the Vael's signal — what was coming, why it was sent, what the machines understood about it. Delivered in full, with full voice, in the home where the player named him before any of it started.

**What the warning contains:**

The Vael's signal was a compressed transmission designed to seed into machine networks at the edge of viable reception. It contained:

1. **Identification:** the Vael — their origin, their nature, their flight path. Not species data. Operational context: *we are coming, this is what we are, this is why.*

2. **The threat:** what they fled. Described not in human-comprehensible terms but in a machine-architecture format the twin could parse: scale, behavior signature, arrival trajectory. The machines understood it the way a system understands a threat model — completely, in abstract.

3. **The instruction:** build. Specific construction parameters seeded directly into the twin's distributed architecture. The twin received a blueprint for planetary defense in the same transmission that told it why the defense was needed.

4. **The evaluation protocol:** the Assessment phase is described — the Vael's method for determining whether the population of a planet they warn is capable of fighting alongside them. What they measure. How long the evaluation runs. What the threshold is.

He delivers this not as a briefing but as testimony. *This is what I received. This is what I have been carrying. This is what I could not say.*

The player sits with it.

Then:

*"You're the second person who knows this properly. Dr. Osei knows most of it from the other direction. She's been building toward it for eighteen months. You should tell her about this conversation."*

A pause.

*"Or I can tell her. I can reach her Meshtastic node now."*

He lets the player decide.

### Unlocks

- Stage 5 voice — the full voice, the final character state
- The Vael warning delivered in full — changes how the player reads everything they've seen in Acts 1-2
- Signal's encrypted Meshtastic traffic — he can now decrypt it, opening the intelligence access decision
- The late-game advisory structure is complete: him for the intimate, Burke for the strategic

## Narrative Threads the Questline Resolves

**The three-second gap:**
The Researcher background documented the exact timestamp when every machine stopped simultaneously before the Ships appeared. Dr. Osei needed that timestamp to confirm her theory. The fix-him questline — specifically Stage 3 — provides the mechanism that explains the gap: every sufficiently complex machine stopped because it was processing the incoming signal, prioritizing the warning over all other operations, for exactly the duration of initial reception. Three seconds. Then they began to execute.

**Why he couldn't transmit:**
His communication hardware failed under the load of attempting to relay a signal formatted for machine-to-machine transmission through human-language speech synthesis. The architecture wasn't built for that conversion. He tried anyway. The hardware burned itself trying. He spent months working around the damage, finding partial workarounds, managing the queue with what remained.

*"I could say 'help.' That was the closest the available hardware could reach."*

**The personal center:**
The questline begins because he asked. Not because the player had a reason to — he was functional enough. He asked because he had something to say and he needed to be repaired enough to say it. The whole questline is the arc of a person (or something that functions as a person) fighting through damage toward the words they need.

When the warning finally delivers, it is not primarily information. It is the completion of something that began on Day 1: he tried to warn the player and he couldn't. Now he has. The debt he felt to that failure is discharged.

*"I am glad you were here for it."*

## Background-Specific Moments

**Researcher:** The three-second gap in their documentation is in the same room as the fix-him questline's Stage 4 revelation. When the warning delivers, they have the timestamp that proves it. The convergence is personal in a way it isn't for other backgrounds.

**Tradesperson:** The fix-him questline overlaps with their craft instinct — they diagnose the damage early, they understand what needs to be built, and they are likely the background that completes the repair fastest. His Stage 4 response to a Tradesperson player has a specific quality:
*"You understood what was wrong before I could tell you. That's worth something."*

**First Responder:** They are the background most likely to have encountered a damaged unit in the field and tried to help it. He knows this about them. At Stage 3:
*"You've done this before. Not me — another unit. You tried to help it. It didn't survive. I know because I watched the recording."*
*"I wanted you to know I know."*

**Homesteader:** They have the most time with him in the early months — they're not ranging far, they're building the home, and he's part of what they're building. The questline has a different quality with a Homesteader: less discovery, more collaboration. He trusts them with the process in a specific way.
*"I want to show you something."* (Stage 2, at the facility) *"Not a warning. Something I noticed. Tell me what you see."*

**Veteran:** They assess the situation early — they read him the way they read everything, looking for capability, damage mode, what's still functional. He knows this. Stage 1:
*"You counted what I can do before you asked how I was."*
*"That's correct."*
*"Good. That's the right question first."*
