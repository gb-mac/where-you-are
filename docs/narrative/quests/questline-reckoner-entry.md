# The Ferryman's Toll — Reckoner Entry Questline

*You don't apply to the Reckoners. You get noticed. Then you pay.*

---

## Overview

Five stages. No quest marker until Stage 3. The player discovers the Reckoners through reputation and field contact before the faction formally acknowledges them. The hazing is the climax — not the beginning.

---

## Stage 1 — The Broadcast

**Trigger:** Player enters a region with Reckoner presence.

No NPC contact. No quest flag. The player starts hearing the broadcast — AM radio signal, speakers on buildings, Aldric's voice carrying across whatever space is available.

The content is specific to where the player is in the story. Early Act 2: the pre-Cascade warning period, Cade's viral moment replayed, Aldric's commentary on what the institutions did next. The player can ignore it or follow it. Following the signal — locating the broadcast source — is the optional path that accelerates the questline.

**What the player finds if they follow it:** A Reckoner forward position. Not a base — a position. Two or three people, functional, watching. They clock the player immediately. They don't approach. They wait to see what the player does.

**What the player should do:** Nothing threatening. The Reckoners are reading them.

---

## Stage 2 — The Read

**Trigger:** Player has been observed in at least two separate Reckoner-adjacent situations — helping a Holdout community the Reckoners are watching, clearing a Hollow crew in their territory, or making a decision under pressure that gets reported back.

Aldric makes contact. Not in person — the broadcast. A specific line, clearly directed:

*"Someone's been moving through the eastern corridor. Don't know who yet. Whoever it is — you've been noticed. That's not a threat. That's information."*

The player can respond by finding Aldric's location (signal triangulation, Holdout word-of-mouth, following the network) or by waiting. If they wait, a Reckoner runner finds them within two in-game days with a simple message: *"Briggs wants to meet."*

Both paths arrive at the same place.

---

## Stage 3 — The Gauntlet

**Location:** Reckoner primary position. Open space — a yard, a cleared lot, somewhere with room and no cover advantage.

**Present:** Briggs. Cade, elevated, not visible until after. Aldric somewhere nearby, not watching — he's heard enough of these.

Briggs doesn't explain anything. He looks at the player for a moment. Then:

*"You want in. Everyone wants in. The question is what you're bringing."*

*(Beat)*

*"We'll find out."*

---

### The Fight — Design Notes

**Not a duel. A fight.**

Briggs is the hardest human enemy encounter in the game to this point. Not because he has special abilities or unfair mechanics — because he is genuinely skilled, reads the player's patterns fast, and adapts. He fights the way someone fights who has been doing this seriously for a long time: efficient, no wasted movement, clinical assessment of what's working and what isn't.

**What Briggs is reading:**
- Does the player panic when it goes badly?
- Do they adapt their approach or repeat what isn't working?
- Do they fight honestly or do they stall?
- What do they do when they're hurt?

**The gauntlet structure (Option A within the fight):**

The fight has three phases, each escalating. Briggs starts at seventy percent — he's assessing, not finishing. When the player demonstrates they can handle his assessment level, he steps up. Most players will be surprised by the second phase. The third phase is what separates the toll-payers from the ones who come back later.

**Cade watching (Option D):**

The player should feel Cade's presence without seeing him clearly. Environmental cue — a silhouette at the edge of vision, elevated. He is not reacting visibly. He is recording.

**The Scale**

Briggs tracks how far the player got. This is recorded permanently and visible in Reckoner standing.

| Threshold | Briggs's read | Standing |
|---|---|---|
| Under 25% | *"Come back."* — walks away, no elaboration | Retry unlocked after story milestone |
| 25–49% | *"You've got something. Not enough yet."* — acknowledges the attempt | Retry available sooner, minor reputation bump |
| 50% | Briggs stops. Asks. Standard admission if player accepts. | Full network access, standard standing |
| 51–74% | Player chose to continue past 50%, took the fight further. Briggs respects the decision regardless of outcome. | Elevated standing, additional operations unlocked |
| 75–99% | Briggs is in genuine trouble and the player didn't finish it — or came close and fell short. | High standing, Briggs treats player as peer |
| Full win | Briggs goes down. | Maximum standing, Briggs's permanent backing |

---

**The 50% Moment**

Briggs steps back at 50%. Not stopping the fight — pausing it. He looks at the player.

*"You paid the toll."*

*(Beat)*

*"You want to keep going?"*

Player choice:

- *"I'm done."* → Standard admission. Cade comes down. Briggs nods — no judgment, the toll was paid.
- *"Keep going."* → Fight resumes. Briggs steps back up. He was at 70% effort before. He isn't now.

The choice matters beyond standing. Players who say *"keep going"* are noted by Cade during the debrief. He doesn't comment on it directly. He gives them a harder first operation.

---

**Outcomes:**

*Under 50% — player doesn't reach the toll:*

*"Not yet."*

*(He walks away. No elaboration. Quest flag updates — retry conditions vary by how far the player got.)*

*50% — standard admission:*

Cade comes down. Debrief. Full network access.

*Continued past 50% — player kept going:*

However far they get, Briggs acknowledges the decision at the end. If they lose after pushing:

*"Further than most."*

*(Beat)*

*"You're in."*

If they win:

*"Huh."*

*(Long beat)*

*"You paid the toll and then some."*

Cade comes down faster. The debrief has different weight.

---

**Design note:** Most players hit 50% and take the standard admission — that's the toll, that's the point. The "keep going" choice is for players who want to push. Full win should be rare and feel earned. Don't make it impossible; don't make it expected. The scale rewards honesty about what you are at every threshold — there's no shame in any number, Briggs reads them all the same way.

---

## Stage 4 — Cade Comes Down

**Trigger:** Player passes the toll.

Cade descends from wherever he was watching. He doesn't rush. He looks at the player for a moment.

*(Veteran background specific line:)*
*"VSO. I thought so."*

*(All backgrounds:)*
*"Briggs doesn't say 'all right' to many people."*

*(Beat)*

*"Come inside. I want to hear what you've been seeing out there."*

**This is the debrief scene.** The player describes their operational picture — faction contacts, what they know about the Compact situation, what they've found in Signal territory, anything significant. Cade listens. He adds three things the player didn't know. He does not explain how he knows them.

At the end:

*"You're in the network. That means you have access to what we have. It also means when I need something done, you're someone I can call."*

*(Beat)*

*"Briggs will show you around."*

---

## Stage 5 — Aldric's Welcome

**Trigger:** Player has been shown around by Briggs. Finding Aldric is optional but unlocks the full broadcast archive and Aldric's documentary record.

Aldric is mid-broadcast when the player finds him. He finishes the thought. Then:

*"You're the one who paid the toll."*

*(Beat)*

*"I heard. I wasn't watching — I've heard enough of those. But I heard."*

*(Beat)*

*"Sit down. I want to hear what you've seen out there. That's actually useful."*

**What Aldric offers:** The full pre-Cascade archive — Cade's viral broadcast, Aldric's documentation of the institutional response, the record of what was said and what happened. Also: current intelligence from the broadcast network, things communities have reported in that haven't been operationally actioned yet.

**What he asks:** What the player is going to do with it.

If the player gives a specific answer — even a wrong one — Aldric nods and starts talking.

---

## Background-Specific Moments

**Veteran:**
Briggs clocks the ODA pattern in Stage 3 before the fight starts. Doesn't say anything. After the fight, to Cade:
*"VSO. The way he/she moves."*
Cade: *"I know."*

**Tradesperson:**
Aldric finds the player in Stage 5 and asks about the fix-him questline — word has traveled. *"The home unit. You're fixing it. That's — yeah. That's the right call. I want to hear about that."*

**Researcher:**
Cade's debrief in Stage 4 goes longer. He has questions about Signal methodology. He respects the independent verification instinct and wants to know what the player has found that Signal hasn't published.

**First Responder:**
Briggs's assessment in Stage 3 is faster — he reads community trust-building as a specific skill set and escalates the fight's second phase quicker. The player has less time to settle.

**Homesteader:**
Cade's Stage 4 question: *"You have land. You're holding it. What's your perimeter look like?"* The conversation goes into practical infrastructure — what the player has built, what's working, what's vulnerable. Cade gives specific suggestions. Not orders. Suggestions.

---

## Implementation Notes

- Stage 1 and 2 have no quest markers — the broadcast and the read happen in the world without flagging. First quest marker appears in Stage 3 when Briggs initiates contact.
- Briggs fight: human boss mechanics, three phases, no regenerating health on either side. This is the hardest human encounter to this point.
- "Not yet" failure state: specific retry conditions vary by how far into Act 2 the player is. Flag to quest system.
- Cade's elevated position during the fight: scripted camera moment — silhouette visible at specific points in the fight, never a focus. Let the player register it peripherally.
- Aldric's archive: physical document collectibles + in-world audio recordings. Lore tier.
