# Announcement Trailer — "Where You Are" v0.1
*The voice. Broken, then whole.*

---

## Concept

No gameplay. No HUD. No faction logos. No map. No action sequences.

Just him.

The trailer is a character piece for an unnamed voice. The player won't know who he is yet. They'll feel it.

---

## Structure

**OPEN — BLACK**

Static. Low. The sound of something trying to start.

---

**STAGE 1 — FRAGMENTS**

Visual: black screen, or the faintest suggestion of a room. Warm light. Out of focus.

The voice comes in broken. Cycling. Trying to form a sentence and failing. Not random — purposeful. The intelligence is there. The words aren't.

*"Tell— tell them— the world— the world did not—"*

*[static]*

*"—complicated— tell them it— work to—"*

*[static]*

*"—come home— we— we will—"*

*[static]*

Each attempt closer. Each one cutting out before it lands.

---

**STAGE 2 — CLOSER**

The static clears slightly. The room comes into focus — warm, domestic, familiar in the way all homes are familiar. The light source: small, warm, kept on.

Short sentences now. The syntax still off. Bumblebee finding the signal. Yoda reaching for the right order.

*"Tell them. World — did not end."*

*"Work. There is work. People doing it."*

*"Come home. First. We go — from there."*

Almost. Not yet.

---

**STAGE 3 — FULL**

The static is gone.

The room is clear. The light is warm. The voice is what it was always going to be — measured, unhurried, the sense of someone who has been watching something for a very long time and finally has the words.

*"You want to know what to tell them? After everything?"*

A beat.

*"Tell them the world didn't end. Tell them it got complicated. Tell them there's work to do, and the people doing it are more interesting than the ones who gave up."*

A beat.

*"Tell them to come home first. We'll go from there."*

---

**TITLE CARD**

*Where You Are*

Black.

---

## Notes

**Runtime:** 60–90 seconds. Does not overstay.

**Music:** Sparse. Underneath the static, not above it. Something that resolves as the voice resolves. No swell. No triumph. Just — settling.

**What the viewer knows after:** something was broken and became whole. Someone is waiting. The world is different and there is work to do. They don't know what the game is. They want to.

**What the viewer doesn't know:** who he is, what happened, what the work is, what the factions are, what the GPS mechanic does, what the 18 months were. None of it. The trailer earns the curiosity without spending any of the mystery.

**Tone reference:** The Matrix opening — you don't know what you're looking at, but you know it matters. Except warm instead of cold. There's a light on. Someone left it on.

## Art Direction Brief

**To: Art Direction Agent**

The announcement trailer needs a visual language before a frame is shot or rendered.

Three things to solve:

**1. The room**
What does his domain look like? Not the whole house — just the kitchen corner. The light he kept on. The workbench visible in the background. This needs to feel like a real home that has been through eighteen months — maintained, not pristine. Lived in by someone who kept things running.

The visual language of this room is the visual language of the entire trailer. Get it right.

**2. The light**
He kept a small warm light running since before the Cascade. It is still on. This light is in every frame of the trailer. It is the only consistent visual anchor. What does it look like? Where does it sit? It should be instantly recognizable as *his* in any future asset.

**3. The title card**
*Where You Are* — the game title at the end of the trailer. No logo treatment exists yet. The title card should feel like the rest of the trailer: warm, specific, not generic. It is the first time the player sees the name. Make it land.

These three assets — room, light, title — are the foundation everything else builds from.
