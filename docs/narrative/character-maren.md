# Maren — Character Document

*"Queen of Diagolon."*

*(She says it without irony and without performance. It's just accurate.)*

---

## Who She Is

Before the Cascade she ran for office. Provincial. Independent candidate, no party backing, no machine behind her. She got enough votes to make the people who were supposed to win uncomfortable, which was the point. She wasn't trying to win. She was trying to demonstrate that the argument had reach — that the thing Cade was saying on stream was being heard by people who didn't normally listen to streams.

She lost. The system worked exactly as she expected it to. She drew the correct conclusion and stopped trying to work inside it.

She met Cade through the broadcasts. Stayed because she saw what he was building and understood it better than he did in one specific way: Cade sees the operational picture. Maren sees the social one. How communities form, what holds them together, what makes people stay when staying gets hard. Cade built a faction. Maren built a movement. The distinction is load-bearing.

She calls herself the Queen of Diagolon. She meant it as a joke once. Now she means it as a description. The flag is real, the territory is real, and someone has to hold the social fabric of it together while Cade runs operations and Briggs runs the standard and Aldric runs the broadcast. That's her. She does it without being asked and without being thanked and she doesn't need either.

---

## What She Does

**The community infrastructure.** The mutual aid network that keeps Reckoner-adjacent Holdout communities functional — supply coordination, skill sharing, the informal economy that moves resources between people who trust each other and doesn't touch the factions that would want a cut. Maren built it. Maren maintains it. It is the reason the Reckoners have goodwill in territory they don't operationally control.

**The narrative.** She knows how the faction reads from the outside. She knows what the Compact says about them, what the Signal analysts write in their reports, what the Covenant's Keepers have filed. She tracks it not because she's worried about reputation — she isn't — but because understanding how you're being framed tells you how you're being targeted. She has been right about incoming pressure three times in the last eighteen months. Cade moved on her read all three times.

**The broadcast supplement.** Aldric runs the main broadcast. Maren runs a separate channel — shorter, more direct, specifically addressed to communities rather than to the movement's core. Where Aldric builds the argument, Maren tells people what to do with it. Practical. Specific. What does this mean for your Holdout community. What does this mean for your supply situation. What do you do next week.

The channel runs recipes and gardening tips alongside the community briefings. Not as filler — as core content. Post-Cascade cooking with available ingredients, preservation techniques, what to grow and when, foraging by region and season. She treats it with the same precision she brings to everything else: correct information, delivered without condescension. People who tune in for the political picture stay for the growing calendar. People who tune in for the growing calendar start paying attention to the political picture. She knows this. It's the same strategy.

The tips are GPS-anchored where possible — what's in season near the player, what grows in their climate zone, what's in Reckoner territory worth foraging. Aldric's broadcast travels. Maren's is local by design.

**The record-correction.** When outside factions — or their affiliated media — misrepresent the Reckoners, Maren responds publicly and specifically. Not defensively. Factually. She cites the original statement, the misrepresentation, the correct version. She does this with a precision that suggests she's been waiting for them to do it wrong. She usually has been.

## Voice

Direct. Faster than Cade, more targeted than Aldric. She doesn't build to the conclusion — she starts there and works backward through the evidence if you need it.

The humor is dry and arrives without warning. She finds the institutional response to the Reckoners genuinely funny — not because it's incompetent, but because it's so legible. She's been watching institutions respond to things for long enough that the pattern is comfortable to her now. Predictable things are manageable things.

She does not perform warmth but she is not cold. The mutual aid network exists because she genuinely cares about the communities in it. She just doesn't announce that. It's visible in what she does, not what she says.

*"They called us a threat to democracy. I ran for office. I participated in the democratic process. I got the democratic result. I drew the democratic conclusion. I'm not sure what part of that they find threatening."*

*"The Compact has a file on Cade. I know what's in it — we got a copy through channels I'm not going to discuss. It's mostly accurate. The parts that are wrong are wrong in a way that tells you exactly what the Compact is afraid of. That's more useful than the accurate parts."*

*"Someone has to make sure people are eating. Cade's not going to do it. Briggs's not going to do it. Aldric will write about it. I'll make sure it happens."*

---

## Her Relationship With Cade

Partners. Not in the soft sense — in the operational sense. They have different pictures of the same situation and they've learned to run both pictures simultaneously without requiring agreement on every detail.

Cade trusts her read on community dynamics the way he trusts Briggs's read on personnel — completely, and without needing it explained. Maren trusts Cade's operational judgment the same way. They disagree in private. In the faction's presence they are a unified position.

What she sees in him that others don't: the weight of being right. Cade carries the pre-Cascade record — everything he said, how it was received, what happened after — with a specific kind of exhaustion that he doesn't show publicly. He's past needing acknowledgment. He's not past the cost of having been ignored. Maren knows the difference. She doesn't address it directly. She accounts for it.

What he sees in her: the thing the faction would lose without her. The Reckoners without Maren are a very capable militia with good comms and a clear diagnosis. With her they're a community. Cade knows the difference. He doesn't say it often. He acts on it constantly.

---

## The Player's Access

Maren is not the first person the player meets in the Reckoner network. She's the second layer — present after the hazing, visible in the community operations, not the one running the intro.

Once the player is in, she's the most consistent point of contact for community-level operations. The mutual aid network runs through her. Players who want to understand the Reckoners' reach beyond their operational territory — the Holdout communities that don't formally align but trust the network — go through Maren.

She will assess the player on one axis: are you going to be useful to the communities in this network, or are you going to extract from them. She reads this faster than Cade does. Her threshold is lower than Briggs's — she's not asking if you can fight. She's asking if you understand what you're fighting for.

Players who pass her read get access to the mutual aid network — a secondary economy that doesn't touch faction systems and has resources other economies can't access. Players who don't pass her read get polite operational assistance and nothing more.

---

## Act 4

Maren is the one who holds the community together when the Act 4 information lands.

Cade updates intellectually. Briggs adapts operationally. Aldric processes it on air. Maren handles the social fracture — the Cleared members who interpret the new information as confirmation that they should burn harder, the Builder members who are trying to figure out what the new frame means for what they've been constructing, the Holdout communities in the network who are scared and need to know what to do.

She does this without drama. She does what she's always done: figures out what people need, makes sure it happens, doesn't announce that she's doing it.

When the player finds her in Act 4 she's mid-operation. She will talk while she works.

*"I know. Cade told me. Aldric's been on air for three hours about it."*

*(Beat)*

*"The communities need to know what this means for them specifically. Not the theory — what do they do next week. That's what I'm working on. You want to help, tell me what you know about the eastern sector's supply situation. That's more useful right now than the cosmological picture."*
