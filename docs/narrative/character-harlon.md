# Harlon Sage — Character Document

*"You want to know what we actually do here? I'll tell you. Most people can't handle it. Let's find out if you're most people."*

---

## Who He Is

Not military. Not federal. The path Landon didn't take and wouldn't have taken — private contractor, bad cop, the grey space between institutions where the work gets done that institutions can't officially do. He has been in that space long enough that it isn't grey to him anymore. It's just where he lives.

He has seen what people do when the structure disappears. He has also seen what people do when the structure is present and looking the other way. He drew the practical conclusion from both and he has not revised it.

He came to the Compact because Landon was building the only thing worth building. He stays because he believes in what's on the other side of the hard part.

That belief is the thing. It is not performance. It is not cover. Harlon Sage genuinely hopes for what the Compact is building — the order, the protection, the civilization that holds. The brutality is the price he has decided is worth paying to get there. Take the hope away and the price has no justification. So the hope is protected. At any cost.

---

## The Distinction from Landon

Landon has conviction. It is structural — the institution is the point, the institution must be held, the institution is what stands between the living and the list.

Harlon has hope. It is personal — he wants the thing to be real, wants the people inside the perimeter to have something worth having, wants the work to mean what it's supposed to mean. He came from a world where nothing meant what it was supposed to mean and he is building something that will.

This makes him more dangerous than Landon in a specific way. Landon you can argue with — the institution can be wrong, the decisions can be reconsidered, the structure can be reformed. Harlon you cannot argue with on the foundation. The hope is not negotiable. Anyone who threatens what the hope is for gets the response that the hope requires.

Landon built the machine. Harlon runs it on the ground. Landon maintains the reasonable surface. Harlon is what the surface is for.

---

## Where He Came From

Private contractor before the Compact — the kind of work that doesn't have a public record. Before that, law enforcement in a city that had already decided what it needed from its police and what it was willing to tolerate, and Harlon had been willing to tolerate a lot before he stopped being willing.

He has been on both sides of the line. He knows what the line costs and what crossing it costs and he has made his calculation. The people who stayed on the line — the ones who kept their hands clean and their conscience clear — he watched what happened to them and to the people they were supposed to protect. He drew the conclusion.

He is not a man who lost his way. He is a man who found a way and followed it to where it goes.

---

## General Zod and the True Believer

Landon has Zod's conviction — the absolute certainty that what he is doing is right, delivered with the authority of a man who has never seriously entertained the alternative.

Harlon has the hope underneath it. He believes in the people inside the perimeter. He believes they deserve what the Compact is building. He believes the cost is worth it because the people paying it — the ones on the wrong end of the checkpoint, the tribunal, the Annexation Protocol — are paying so that the people inside can have something real.

He is not cynical about this. Cynicism would be easier. Harlon Sage is a man with genuine faith in a brutal project and that is a specific and difficult thing to be.

The darkness is not separate from the hope. It is downstream of it. He does what he does *because* he believes. That's the thing the player has to sit with.

---

## What He Does

He runs the operations Landon can't run officially. The enforcement that doesn't go in the report. The conversations that end problems before they become problems. The communities that needed to understand what the Annexation Protocol meant in practice.

He is also — and this is what makes him complex — the one who holds the line against the Compact's worst elements. The officer who goes too far gets Harlon's attention too. Not because Harlon is merciful — because Harlon has standards, and the standards exist to protect what he's building. Cruelty without purpose is waste. He does not tolerate waste.

This means the player will occasionally see Harlon do something that looks like justice. It is not justice. It is quality control.

---

## His Relationship with the Player

He reads people fast and accurately — occupational requirement, two decades of it. The player gets assessed in the first conversation and Harlon does not revise his assessment easily.

He will tell the player things Landon won't. Not because he's loose — because he thinks the truth is the point. The player who can handle it earns a specific kind of standing with Harlon. Not warmth. Utility. He uses people he respects harder than people he doesn't.

*"You want to know what we actually do here? I'll tell you. Most people can't handle it. Let's find out if you're most people."*

If the player passes: he is direct, specific, occasionally useful in ways Landon isn't. He will tell you what the operation actually costs and what it actually produces. He does not soften it.

If the player doesn't pass: he is not hostile. He simply recalibrates what he shares with them. They become a tool rather than a person. He treats tools carefully — they're useful.

---

## The Negan Register

The ideology worn openly. The masculine duty, the protection frame, the strength as organizing principle — he doesn't hold it behind the institution. He says it directly, without apology, and means every word.

*"You don't protect what belongs to you, sooner or later it belongs to someone else. That's not philosophy. That's Tuesday."*

*"A man provides. Even when he's not appreciated. Even when he's not respected. Even when he's not loved. He bears up and he does it. That's the job. Nobody's coming to give you credit for it."*

*"Chaos isn't a pit. It's a ladder. Most people won't climb it because they're afraid of what they'll have to do. I'm not afraid of what I'll have to do."*

Dark humor, specific, landing hard. He finds the absurdity in things — not the way Aldric does, with distance and commentary, but with the recognition of a man who has been in the room where the absurd thing happened and understands that the only alternative to laughing is something worse.

---

## The Arc

Harlon Sage does not have a redemption arc. That would require him to be wrong about something he's not wrong about, or to want something he doesn't want.

What he has is a reckoning arc. At some point — late Act 3, early Act 4 — the thing he hoped for comes into contact with what the Compact has actually become, and the gap between them is visible even to him. The hope is still there. The thing it was for is not what he was building toward.

He does not break. He recalibrates. What he does with the recalibration is one of the game's significant choices — for him and for the player.

---

## Voice

Direct. No filler. Finds the thing other people won't say and says it without performance. The dark humor is real — he has genuinely found the world funny in a specific way for a long time.

Physical presence — the kind of stillness that comes from a man who has been in enough situations to know that movement before it's needed is information given away.

*"People always want to know if I sleep at night. I sleep fine. Always have. The people who don't sleep at night are the ones who haven't decided yet. I decided a long time ago."*

*"Landon builds things. I make sure they hold. Different skill set."*

*"The thing about the line — everybody's got one. The question is whether yours is in a useful place."*
