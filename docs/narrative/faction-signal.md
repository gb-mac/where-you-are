# The Signal — Faction Document

*"Someone has to figure out what actually happened. It might as well be us."*

---

## What They Are

Not a faction by design. A network by necessity.

The Signal formed from the people who looked at the Cascade and decided the correct response was comprehension, not survival. Scientists, analysts, hackers, intelligence operatives, journalists — people whose entire prior life had been about understanding how systems work. The Cascade was a system. It had mechanisms. The mechanisms could be studied.

Nobody founded the Signal. It crystallized around a shared conviction: the Cascade is comprehensible, and nobody in a position of power is trying hard enough to comprehend it. The Iron Compact wants to control the machines. The Covenant wants to ascend to them. The Reckoners want to name the failure. The Signal wants to understand what actually happened and what is actually coming.

They are not naive about what they're doing. They know the information is dangerous. They know the factions would use it badly. They share it anyway — selectively, at a price, to the people they believe will do something useful with it.

## The Five

No hierarchy. No leadership structure. The Signal is a network of specific people who trust each other enough to share what they know and distrust each other enough to never give anyone the complete picture.

**Dr. Amara Osei** — the theorist. Cellular biology background, post-Cascade pivot to machine behavior. Has a theory about the Coherent construction projects that is consistent with all available data and too dangerous to publish. She is the Signal's intellectual anchor and the one most likely to be right about the thing nobody wants to be right about. → `character-osei.md`

**Alastor** — the burned asset. Former intelligence, handler unknown, institutional affiliation classified and probably no longer relevant. Was burned by the agency or burned the agency — the story changes depending on who's telling it. Off-leash, no constraints, the most socially capable person in the Signal and the one you'd least want to trust with something you couldn't afford to lose. Charming in the way of someone who learned charm as a tool and never stopped. → `character-alastor.md`

**Julian Lovelace** — the scientist. Pre-Cascade research in distributed systems and machine learning infrastructure — work that turns out to be directly relevant to what the Coherent are building. He didn't know what he was building toward. He does now. Carries that knowledge the way you carry something that weighs more every year. → `character-lovelace.md`

**PR0PH3CY** — the hacker. The handle is the identity — no other name offered, none accepted. Was connecting the dots years before the Cascade, publishing to forums where nobody believed them, accumulating a record that reads like prophecy in retrospect. Has the receipts for everything. The most paranoid person in the Signal and the most likely to be right about something the others haven't caught yet. → `character-prophecy.md`

**Archangel** — naval intelligence. Callsign only. The most connected and the least readable. Shows up with information that shouldn't be possible to have and never explains the source. The others have learned not to ask. Finds the player when the player becomes relevant — not before. May never give a real name. → `character-archangel.md`

## What They Do

- Translate and analyze recovered Vael Probe data
- Map Coherent construction projects and identify the pattern
- Document Fractured behavior and accumulation rates — the most complete Fractured database in existence
- Maintain an encrypted, low-power, distributed communication network
- Study the Touched — what contact actually does, biologically and neurologically
- Collect, cross-reference, and sit on information that other factions would use badly

Signal questlines are investigation missions. Find the thing, document it correctly, get it back. Combat is incidental. Understanding is the objective. They pay in information — Assessment Data translations, Fractured profiles, Coherent project maps, Vael pattern schedules. The currency of comprehension.

---

## Ground Texture

No fixed base. Nodes — a university basement with the right equipment, a server rack in a Holdout community whose leadership asked no questions, a converted shipping container three kilometers from the nearest settlement. Each node is one person or two. Each person knows two or three others in the network. Nobody has the full picture by design.

The aesthetic is functional paranoia. Everything is low-power, low-signature, deniable. The equipment is salvaged and modified. The locations are chosen for exit routes first, working conditions second. They have been compromised before and they have moved before and they will move again.

Meeting Signal members in the field: they find you, mostly. They have been watching. They know what you've been doing. They make contact when the cost-benefit calculation comes out in favor of contact. The player who has been doing the right things in the right places will notice they're being noticed before anyone introduces themselves.

## What They Know and Won't Say Yet

The Signal's information asymmetry is the most significant in the game. They know more about the Cascade's actual mechanisms than any other faction. They are not sharing all of it.

Dr. Osei's unpublished theory about the Coherent construction is the centerpiece — consistent with all available data, too dangerous to release because if she's right, every faction's current strategy is not just wrong but counterproductive. This is the Act 3 information that reframes everything.

Archangel knows something else. What it is and when it surfaces is the Act 4 accelerant.

## Relationships

**Iron Compact** — the Compact wants to conscript Signal technical skills. Signal hides from them. The Compact knows Signal exists and has a file. The file is incomplete by design. Some Compact Pragmatists are quietly Signal-adjacent — they want the same understanding, for different reasons.

**Covenant** — epistemic rivals. Both studying the same phenomena, reaching opposite conclusions. The Signal finds the Covenant's theological framework intellectually sloppy. The Covenant finds the Signal's refusal to accept meaning emotionally arid. They share data occasionally, under sufferance, when the alternative is both knowing less than they need to. Dr. Osei and the Covenant's Keepers are the most likely point of contact.

**Reckoners** — Aldric is Signal-adjacent without being Signal. He reports what they find before they publish it, which they find irritating and occasionally useful. Cade's faction has the operational reach the Signal lacks. Occasional collaboration, no formal relationship.

**Holdouts** — depend on Holdout hospitality for nodes and safe houses. Pay in local intelligence and advance warning of threats. The most reliable non-hostile relationship in the Signal's world.

**Wardens** — mutual respect, cautious. The Wardens' Record and the Signal's documentation overlap in useful ways. Neither faction fully trusts the other's methodology.

## The Player

The Signal doesn't recruit. They identify.

The player who has been paying attention — to the Coherent construction, to Fractured behavior patterns, to the Vael Probe schedules — will start to notice they're being watched. Small things. Information that arrives before it should. A dead drop with something in it they needed. A codec call from a voice that knows more about their last three missions than anyone should.

They are being assessed. The assessment has been running since before first contact.

When they finally meet — Alastor first, probably, because Alastor is the one who manages first contact — the Signal already knows what the player is. The question is whether the player is ready to know what the Signal knows.
