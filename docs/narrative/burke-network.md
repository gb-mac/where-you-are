# Burke's Network — The Responsibles
*"I've been watching your record. The real one, not the AR overlay. What you chose when it wasn't convenient. What you held when holding was expensive."*

---

## What It Is

Not a faction. Not an institution. Not a formal structure of any kind.

Burke has been quietly identifying specific people across the post-Cascade world and placing them where they need to be. Not recruiting — positioning. A Compact Pragmatist officer in the right assignment rather than the one the Hardliner bloc wanted. A Signal analyst with specific knowledge working in a region where that knowledge matters. A Warden Sworn with cross-faction credibility placed on a Moot where a decision is going wrong. A Holdout community leader who was going to be removed by the community's internal politics, kept in place through a single well-timed intervention nobody formally acknowledges.

He does this without announcement. The people he positions mostly don't know they've been positioned — they know someone made something possible, and if they know Burke, they know enough not to ask for the details.

The network's organizing principle is his framework: Responsibles. People who are oriented toward the work, toward what can be built with what they have, toward stewardship rather than grievance. In the pre-Cascade world he would have called them builders — entrepreneurs, community anchors, people who showed up and held things together not because they were paid to but because the alternative was watching something fall apart.

In the post-Cascade world they're harder to find and more important than they've ever been. He has been finding them.

---

## How It Works

The network has no structure. It is a set of bilateral relationships, each one built on trust, each one maintained through value exchange. Burke provides: information, context, the specific clarity of someone who has been watching the whole picture since Month 1. His network members provide: their work, their positions, and — when the moment comes — a specific action in a specific place.

He doesn't call in the network except when the framework itself is at risk. Not faction politics. Not territorial disputes. Not resource conflicts. Those are downstream consequences of the same upstream failures he has been documenting for twenty years. He doesn't spend network capital on the symptoms.

The threshold is: conditions under which civilization cannot be rebuilt at all. There are specific threat profiles that trigger the threshold. He has a list. He has thought about it for a long time.

---

## Who Is In It

The network is not large. Burke has no interest in managing a large network — that becomes an institution, and institutions drift. He maintains approximately thirty bilateral relationships at any given time. Each one is chosen with the same precision he applies to everything.

**The Compact Pragmatist cluster:**

Three to five officers across different regions, each of whom has demonstrated that they run the Compact's mission as stated rather than the Hardliner interpretation of it. He monitors the Hardliner/Pragmatist power balance and has, at specific moments, passed information to Pragmatist officers that changed which way a regional command transition went.

He does not pick sides in the Compact's internal war. He picks functionality. The Hardliner faction will eventually collapse on its own institutional logic; he is making sure the Pragmatist capability survives the collapse.

**The Signal analysts:**

Two researchers with specific knowledge of the Coherent construction data and the Vael behavioral patterns. They are not Dr. Osei — he leaves that relationship unclaimed, available to grow on its own terms. These are the people one tier below her, who would continue the work if something happened to her. He has made sure they're in positions where they can.

He has also made sure they talk to each other. Regularly. Across Signal cells that wouldn't normally exchange data at that level. The knowledge is distributed because distributed knowledge survives.

**The Warden cross-faction contacts:**

Three Sworn with established credibility outside Warden-affiliated communities — people who can walk into an Iron Compact zone and be received as a witness, or carry a message to a Covenant Keeper without triggering the Vigil's concern, or negotiate at a Holdout community table that won't talk to Compact or Signal.

The Wardens have Arbiters who fulfill this role formally. Burke's contacts do it informally, which means they can do things an Arbiter can't without putting the Record at risk.

**The Holdout anchors:**

Five community leaders in five distinct regions, each of whom has demonstrated sustained competence under pressure and the specific quality Burke values above everything else: they make decisions that are bad for them personally when those decisions are good for their community. The self-sacrifice signal. Not martyrdom — the willingness to absorb personal cost without advertising it.

He keeps them resourced. Quietly. Things appear when these communities need them — not from Burke directly, but from people who know Burke, routed in ways that don't create dependency.

**The independent operators:**

A rotating group of three to five people with specific capabilities and no faction affiliation — people who can go places and do things that faction-affiliated members can't, because they don't carry the faction flag. The player, if they've built the right Record, is one of these. In the late game.

## The Player's Position

Burke identifies the player as a Responsible based on their Record. Not their faction standing — their actual Record. What they chose when the choice was hard. What they held when holding was expensive.

He doesn't announce this. He has been watching since their first visit to his property. The conversations they've had were not tests — he wasn't administering something. He was observing. The observation accumulated into a conclusion.

In late Act 3 or Act 4, after a major decision the player has navigated — the publication question, a faction conflict with significant consequences, a Warden code moment with weight — Burke stops asking questions.

*"I've been watching your record. The real one, not the AR overlay. What you chose when it wasn't convenient. What you held when holding was expensive. You're one of the Responsibles. Rare enough that I'm not going to waste the observation.*

*Here's what I need from you. Here's what it costs. Here's why it matters.*

*You can say no. But you won't."*

What he asks for is specific. Not "join my network" — that's not how it works. A specific action in a specific place at a specific time. One of the network's critical positions is at risk — a Compact Pragmatist officer about to be removed, a Signal analyst whose location has been compromised, a Holdout community whose anchor is being isolated by the community's own politics. He needs someone who can go there and do something that the person in his network can't do alone.

The player does the thing. It costs something — time, faction standing, risk. It matters — the cascading consequences are visible in Act 4. Something that would have broken is still standing.

He doesn't offer formal network membership after. He just knows them now. The relationship is permanent and the specific weight of the obligation is real: he will ask again, at some point, and the player will have to decide again.

## The Framework Threat Trigger

Burke moves — actually moves, acts directly rather than positioning others to act — when the framework itself is at risk.

The framework is the set of conditions under which civilization can be rebuilt: rule of something approximating law, functional supply chains, communities that can trust their neighbors enough to trade, a minimal information commons, the survival of enough institutional knowledge to reconstruct what was lost.

He has been monitoring the framework's status since Month 1. He has a mental model of its key dependencies and its key vulnerabilities. He watches the Compact's kill chain position specifically — he has identified that the Hardliner faction's trajectory, if unchecked, terminates in conditions that make regional civilization impossible. Not because the Hardliners are evil. Because the institutional failure mode they represent produces outcomes they don't intend.

The threshold event is something that would cascade to framework collapse within a timeframe Burke can model. Not a faction loss, not a resource crisis — those are within the framework's tolerance. The trigger is something structural: a specific Compact power transition that locks in Hardliner control regionally, a Signal network compromise that removes the information commons entirely, a Warden Accord fracture that removes the only cross-faction accountability mechanism.

When one of those happens, or when the conditions that produce them are locked in:

Burke calls in the network. All of it. Simultaneously.

He has not done this in eighteen months. Every member of the network knows it is possible. None of them has been told what the threshold is — only that it exists and that when he calls, it means it has been reached.

What the network does when activated: a set of coordinated, positioned actions that individually look unrelated and collectively prevent the cascade. Not a coup. Not a military operation. A series of interventions — information passed, resources redirected, decisions influenced — that change the conditions producing the failure before the failure locks in.

It works because it is not centralized. No single failure point. Each member acts in their own operational area, and the outcomes don't add up to anything legible unless you know the framework model.

Burke is the only one who knows the framework model. That is the vulnerability and the strength.

---

## Burke and the Player at the End

If the player is in the network and the threshold event occurs, they receive a message from Burke unlike any other he has sent. Not the codec call style. Not the long analytical frame.

Short. Specific. Operational.

*"The threshold is reached. You know the location. What you do when you get there is yours. I'll tell you what I know about the conditions. The rest is your judgment.*

*I trust it."*

That is the end of the arc for most players who have built the Burke relationship. Not a climax — a confirmation. He identified them as a Responsible early, he watched the Record confirm it across Acts 1–3, and now he is trusting his analysis.

If they've been paying attention, they already know what to do.
