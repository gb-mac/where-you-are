# Character Creation — "Where You Are" v0.1
*Who you were before. Who you're looking for. Who you become.*

---

## Philosophy

There is no character creation screen. There is no stat allocation. There are no classes.

Instead, the game asks one question before you play:

**"Before all this — what were you?"**

You pick your background. The game shows you sixty seconds of who that person was. Somewhere in those sixty seconds you name your assistant. Then the world breaks and you are that person, standing in that life, trying to survive what just happened to it.

Your background is the starting shape. The Record is what you build from it.

---

## The Intro Video

Each background triggers a distinct sixty-second intro video. These are not cutscenes — they are glimpses of a normal morning in the specific life you're about to inhabit.

**What every intro video contains:**
- Your home environment (reflecting background and GPS location type)
- Your assistant, doing what it was built to do
- Your morning routine — specific to who you are
- One interactive moment: the assistant's activation/naming sequence appears as part of the footage. A prompt surfaces in-world: *"What did you name your assistant?"* The player types it. The assistant learns it. The game keeps it.
- The ending: always the same. The alarm cycling wrong sounds. The assistant already different in the corner. The world already tilting. You wake up into it.

**Tone:** Warm, specific, ordinary. Two minutes of ordinary is the most effective setup for everything that follows. The intro video is the emotional anchor the whole game pulls against. This is what was lost — not civilization in the abstract, but that specific morning.

## The Five Backgrounds

---

### The Tradesperson
*Mechanic, electrician, carpenter — someone who builds and fixes things.*

**The sixty seconds:** A garage workshop before dawn. Coffee going. A vehicle half-assembled on a lift or a project on the workbench. The assistant reminding them about a parts order or a job scheduled for the morning. Practical, quiet, competent. This person knows how things are built. The world breaking is, on some level, a problem they can work on.

**Day 1 reality:** The tools in the garage are immediately useful. The automated equipment they installed themselves — the home systems, the smart tools — are now open questions. The Cascade hits differently when you understand the machines.

**Starting conditions:**
- Tool kit (crowbar, wrench, utility knife, work gloves)
- Repair knowledge — field repairs faster than any other background, always
- Improvised weapon proficiency — they know what hits hard and what breaks
- A vehicle in some state of repair (depends on GPS location, may not be functional Day 1)

**Persistent edge:** Crafting and repair speed. The Tradesperson can fabricate and fix gear in the field at a level no other background reaches without significant investment. Fractured machine components are more legible to them — they can read an accumulation tier and make gear from the parts.

**Natural affinity:** Holdout (immediate practical value to any community), early Separated rapport (machines make sense to them; the Separated recognize someone who doesn't fear what they don't understand)

---

### The First Responder
*EMT, firefighter, nurse, officer — trained to run toward the thing everyone else runs from.*

**The sixty seconds:** End of a shift or beginning of one. Uniform. The specific rhythm of someone who has a schedule that exists to serve other people. The assistant managing the calendar, reminding about handoff notes or shift start. Community ties visible even in sixty seconds — this person knows their neighborhood because their job required it.

**Day 1 reality:** The instinct is to go toward it. That's both an asset and a liability in the first week. But they know the police station. They know the community center. They know which neighbors have medical needs. They move through the early chaos with more information than anyone else.

**Starting conditions:**
- Basic first aid kit (better wound treatment from Day 1)
- Community knowledge — NPCs in Holdout communities extend initial trust faster
- Police station familiarity — layout known, access easier
- Basic combat awareness from the officer variant (or medical proficiency from EMT/nurse)

**Persistent edge:** Community trust accrues faster across all human factions. Authority situations — checkpoints, Compact tribunals, Warden hearings — navigate more naturally. First Responders are recognized as people who kept showing up, and that recognition persists.

**Natural affinity:** Holdout (community protection instinct), Iron Compact (institutional alignment — they understand chain of command and it understands them)

---

### The Researcher
*Academic, analyst, scientist — needs to understand before they can act.*

**The sixty seconds:** Late night or early morning at a desk covered in work. Screens, notes, the specific organized chaos of someone deep in a problem. The assistant reading them a summary or managing references while they work. This person's relationship with the world is mediated through analysis. The Cascade is, to some part of them, a problem to be solved — and that's both their strength and what makes the first week hardest.

**Day 1 reality:** Less physically prepared than most. The tools of their life — data, networks, equipment — are disrupted. But the habit of documentation kicks in immediately, and what they document in the first weeks becomes Signal intelligence before the Signal exists.

**Starting conditions:**
- Laptop (partially functional — local storage intact, network gone)
- Documentation habit — naturally generates data that unlocks Signal access faster
- Machine behavior pattern recognition — Fractured accumulation tiers readable sooner
- Signal network access earlier than other backgrounds

**Persistent edge:** Lore and intelligence unlocks accelerate throughout the game. The Researcher reads the world's data layer faster — Vael behavioral patterns, Coherent construction correlations, faction communications. Signal standing grows faster. Dr. Osei recognizes them and trusts them sooner.

**Natural affinity:** Signal (natural fit — they were already doing Signal work before the Signal existed), slight Covenant curiosity (the Cascade is a theological question and an empirical one simultaneously)

---

### The Veteran
*Military background — a different relationship with threat from the first moment.*

**The sixty seconds:** A disciplined morning. Specific, efficient, unhurried. The assistant set to a tight schedule. The home reflects someone who lives with structure — not austere, but organized. This person has seen operational chaos. Day 1 reads like a combat environment, and that recognition is the most valuable thing they own in the first week.

**Day 1 reality:** The threat assessment works. The tactical movement works. The police station run that nearly kills other backgrounds is hard but manageable. The weight is different — the Veteran knows what comes after the first week, and part of them has been waiting for something like this. That knowledge is not comfortable.

**Starting conditions:**
- Tactical gear (load-bearing, durable)
- Weapons proficiency — combat system unlocks available earlier
- Threat assessment — readable machine behavior from Day 1, faster than any other background
- Physical conditioning — wound penalties reduced in the first weeks

**Persistent edge:** Combat system depth unlocks earliest. The Veteran's full capability in the Arkham/Gun Fu system is available before other backgrounds reach it. Iron Compact interactions start at a higher baseline — they recognize their own.

**Natural affinity:** Iron Compact (institutional alignment — the Compact is organized around people like this), Warden (the code resonates with military discipline — keep your word, know the rules of engagement, conduct matters)

---

### The Homesteader
*Farmer, off-grid prepper, rural self-sufficient — the most prepared and the most blindsided simultaneously.*

**The sixty seconds:** Before dawn, already working. Land visible through the window or door — fields, outbuildings, space. The assistant managing planting schedules, weather data, equipment maintenance. This person has thought about collapse. Not this collapse — they had the wrong mental model — but they prepared for grid failure, supply chain breakdown, social disruption. The stores are full. The well is functional. The land is productive.

**Day 1 reality:** The food and water runway is longer than any other background. The weapons are there. The knowledge of how to sustain is deep. And their own property is now trying to kill them — the tractor, the irrigation automation, the grain auger. The thing that prepared them for collapse is also their first threat. They have to get through their own yard before anything else.

**Starting conditions:**
- Food stores (survival system starts with significantly more runway — days, not hours)
- Water source (well or tank — not dependent on municipal supply)
- Weapons (rural ownership — rifle or shotgun from Day 1)
- Land and tools for food production
- Knowledge: seasons, preservation, sustainability — the growing food system unlocks immediately

**Persistent edge:** The Homesteader never fully runs out of food production capacity as long as they maintain access to land. The survival system's food track is always more manageable. In the late game, a Homesteader with established land is a community resource — their property can feed a Holdout settlement. The Warden code recognizes them: landowner, steward, someone who kept something running.

**Natural affinity:** Holdout (the self-sufficiency model is what Holdout communities are trying to build — a Homesteader is already there), Warden (the landowner/steward origin of the Houses is exactly this person — they may be invited toward the Accord earlier than other backgrounds)

## The Gap Closes

Backgrounds matter most in the first month — the hardest stretch, when the specific things you know and have determine whether you survive it.

By Month 3 any background can develop in any direction. The persistent edges stay — the Tradesperson always repairs faster, the Healthcare Worker always has better wound outcomes — but they are advantages, not walls. A Veteran can become the best field medic in the zone. A Researcher can become lethal. A Homesteader can build a Holdout community that feeds a city.

The background is the starting shape. The Record is everything after.

## The Assistant

Every background has one. Every intro video has one. Every player names one.

The naming happens inside the intro video, as part of the assistant's activation sequence — a prompt that surfaces in-world rather than in a menu. The player types the name. The assistant learns it. The game keeps it.

The assistant knows if it was named immediately or if the player hesitated. It knows if it was named something affectionate or something functional. It has been called that name for however many years the character has owned it.

It will be relevant. More relevant than it looks on Day 1.

For the assistant's full arc — Day 1 through late game — see `quests/prologue-day-one.md`.

## The Personal Arc — "Is There Someone You're Looking For?"

After the background is selected and the intro video plays, before the game begins, one optional question:

**"Is there someone you're looking for?"**

Not required. If the player skips it, the game proceeds. If they answer:

A second prompt: *Who?* — relationship type (partner, sibling, parent, friend, neighbor) and one identifying detail. A name.

This generates a thread that runs through the eighteen months underneath everything else. It surfaces organically:
- An NPC saw someone matching that description heading in a specific direction
- A Signal network record has a name that matches
- A Holdout community took in a group from that area — were they in it?
- A Warden Marker was placed at a location connected to them
- A Covenant community has someone who arrived alone and won't say from where

The arc can resolve in reunion. In finding out they didn't make it. In finding them changed — Compact, Covenant, Hollow, Riven — and having to decide what that means now.

It runs like the excommunicado arc: always present in the background, intersecting with the main story at specific beats, never blocking it.

**The distinction:**
- The excommunicado arc is about who you *became* — conduct, consequence, the code
- The personal arc is about who you were *trying to get back to* — connection, loss, the before
- The Record is who you *are* — documented, witnessed, permanent

Three axes. One player story.

---

## What This System Is Not

Not a class system with locked abilities. Not an alignment system. Not a morality meter.

It is a starting point and a question. The background shapes the first month. The personal arc shapes the emotional stakes. The Record shapes everything — who you helped, what you kept, what you became when the structures that defined you were gone.

The Vael's Assessment measured all of it.

The answer is in the Record.
