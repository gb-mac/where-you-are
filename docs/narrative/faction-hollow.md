# The Hollow — Faction Document

*"There is no philosophy. There is what you can take and what you can't."*

---

## What They Are

The Hollow are what happens when people decide the social contract didn't survive the Cascade and they'd rather be the ones writing the new terms.

Not one group. A hundred groups with a shared behavioral logic: take what you can, hold what you take, sell or use the rest. Organized crime that was already operating before the Cascade and simply stopped pretending otherwise. Military units that went rogue. Survivalist groups whose philosophy curdled. People who lost enough — family, community, hope — that predation started to feel like the only rational response.

They are the most recognizably human faction. They have humor, relationships, things they love and protect. They just operate entirely outside any ethical framework that includes people they haven't already decided matter.

---

## The Four Tiers

**Scavengers** — lowest organization. Small bands, no fixed territory, opportunistic. Will rob you if they can. Will run if they can't. Mostly manageable.

**Crews** — mid-tier. Established territory, consistent leadership, something resembling rules — mostly around who gets to do what to whom within the group. Brutal to outsiders, functional internally. Can be negotiated with if you have something they want.

**Syndicates** — former organized crime operations that became the dominant power structure in urban zones when institutional pressure disappeared. Supply chains, enforcers, intelligence networks, political relationships with other factions. The Iron Compact officially hunts them. Compact regional commanders sometimes quietly do business with them. Syndicates can be more reliable partners than their reputation suggests — they understand contracts.

**The Riven** — Zephyr's tier. Not criminal — devoted. Full faction entry at `character-zephyr.md`.

## The Syndicate System — GPS-Anchored

The Hollow Syndicates are not globally named. They are regionally generated.

The Cascade didn't create the Syndicates — it removed the institutional pressure that was containing them. What emerges in each region reflects what was already there: gang territory, ethnic demographics, neighborhood history, the specific organized crime infrastructure that existed before the lights went out. MS-13 presence in one corridor. Triad operations in another. Outlaw MC territory in rural areas. The player's local Hollow landscape reflects their actual location.

**The system:**
- OSM + demographic data identifies probable Syndicate territory by region
- Named operators seeded per region from a culturally-appropriate pool
- Faction territory boundaries pulled from real gang territory data where available
- Same behavioral logic everywhere — different operators, different aesthetics, different leverage

Someone playing in LA encounters different Syndicate texture than someone in Toronto or Glasgow. The Hollow is the faction where the named characters anchor to real geography.

**What this means for the player:** The Hollow they meet is their Hollow. The operator running the corridor they grew up near has a specific history with that place. The Cascade changed the power structure. It didn't change the territory.

## The Syndicate Operators

No globally named bosses. Regional figures, each a different flavor of the same thing.

**The archetypes the pool draws from:**

*The Businessperson* — organized, reliable if you have something worth trading, has been running operations since before the Cascade and treats the Compact's collapse as a business opportunity rather than a crisis. Understands contracts. Will hold to deals as long as they serve. The most functional Hollow operator and the most dangerous to underestimate — they're not chaotic, they're calculating.

*The Volatile* — unpredictable, useful for exactly that reason. Has taken more territory through intimidation than through force because nobody wants to find out what happens when they stop being unpredictable. Player can work with them on high-risk operations where deniability matters. Cannot be trusted between sessions.

*The Local* — has been running the same block since before the Cascade. Knows every angle, every history, every old debt. The Cascade changed the power structure. Didn't change the territory. The Local is the territory. Deep roots, deep knowledge, specific and personal relationships with every Holdout community in range.

*The Ideological Bleed* — a Syndicate operator who has absorbed Riven thinking without joining the Riven. The devotion is present but the structure is criminal. The most dangerous combination: organized predation with a philosophical justification. Zephyr finds them useful and disturbing in equal measure.

## The Ideological Tier — Cross-Faction Bleed

The Riven are the most visible ideological infection but the ideology doesn't stay in the Riven.

Zephyr's proof — that every system of order is organized violence with better paperwork — travels. It finds the cracks in every faction and settles there.

**Compact Hardliners** who've read too much Zephyr: the devotion to order that tips from conviction into something that looks like the thing Zephyr is demonstrating. They would be horrified to be called Riven-adjacent. The structure of the belief is the same.

**Covenant expelled members**: the Covenant expels people who go too far toward the Ascendant end without institutional sanction. Some of them find the Riven. The theological remnants produce hybrid belief systems — the devotion to transition merged with the devotion to chaos. The Riven that result are the most operationally unpredictable.

**Holdout communities** that have been under enough pressure long enough: the performance of devotion around resistance. Not criminal, not ideological in Zephyr's sense, but the behavioral pattern is identical — a community that has built identity around what it opposes rather than what it holds. Vulnerable to Riven recruitment. The player can see it coming before it happens.

**Signal-adjacent independents** who followed the receipts too far: PR0PH3CY is the functional version of this. The person who was right about everything and arrived at Zephyr's diagnosis from an analytical direction. Most of them stop before Zephyr's conclusion. A few don't.

The ideological tier is not a faction. It is a pattern. It appears where the structures fail to provide what people need and the resentment has been building long enough to find a voice.

Zephyr didn't invent it. He named it and gave it direction. Without him it would still be happening — just less coherently.

## What They Offer Players

Syndicates have inventory. Everything they've taken from everyone else. Access to goods from across the region — including things no other faction has. They also know routes. They move constantly, and they know how to move without being stopped.

Players who can deal with Hollow Crews or Syndicates get:
- Black market goods, faction-neutral equipment, rare salvage
- Route intelligence — how to move without being seen
- Deniable operational support — the Compact can't officially sanction what the player does with a Hollow crew
- Regional intelligence that no other faction has because no other faction goes where the Hollow go

---

## What They Do to Players They Can't Deal With

Anything they want. The Hollow have no rules about outsiders.

Players with high Displacement History are noted. The Hollow watch for patterns, and someone who keeps reappearing in the same city from far away is someone who can be separated from their support network. This is a strategic observation, not a philosophical one.
