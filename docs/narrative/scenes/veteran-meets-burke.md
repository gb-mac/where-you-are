# Scene: The Veteran Meets Burke
*Act 1 / Act 2 threshold. Available to Veteran background players only.*

---

## How They Find Him

The Veteran doesn't find Burke through a Signal database or a faction introduction. They find him the way ODA teams found local assets in unfamiliar environments: by asking the right people the right questions, noticing which names come up without being volunteered, and following the thread.

Two weeks of operations in the region. The Veteran has been building their assessment — who has standing, where the real resource flows go, what the actual authority structure is underneath the nominal one. They've talked to Compact conscripts, Holdout community leaders, a Warden Sworn, two Signal contacts. Different factions, different incentives. Same pattern: a name that comes up when someone is explaining something complicated, not as a faction resource, not as a contact — more as a reference point. *Burke knows about that.* *You should ask Burke.* *I don't know why it works that way, it just does.*

Nobody sends them to him. Nobody vouches for them. They track the name until they have an address and then they show up.

The property is three generations deep. That's visible from the gate. Someone has been maintaining this land in a specific way — not just survival maintenance, but care. The garden is productive in a way that suggests someone has been thinking about soil, not just harvest. The perimeter is secure in a way that doesn't announce itself. The whole property has the quality of somewhere that was prepared for this.

Burke is in the yard when the Veteran arrives. He looks at them for a moment.

*"I wondered when someone was going to track that down."*

He goes back to what he was doing.

*"Come in."*

---

## The First Hour

He doesn't offer them a mission or a briefing. He offers them a seat and starts asking questions.

Not personal questions. Operational ones.

*"What did you make of the Compact checkpoint on the 16th Street corridor?"*

The Veteran answers. Burke listens without comment.

*"And the Holdout at the school — when you met the community leader, did she have the room or did she have the standing? There's a difference."*

The Veteran has noticed this. They say so. They describe what they saw.

Burke puts down what he's working on and looks at them directly for the first time.

*"Both. You clocked both. Most people only get one. They either read the room — who's loud, who's deferred to — or they read the standing — the quiet person everyone looks at when the loud person finishes talking. You got both in the same read.*

*That's the ODA model. Where did you serve?"*

The Veteran tells him. He nods — not impressed, registering.

*"I did mine later. Forty-two years old when I enlisted. People thought I was having some kind of crisis. I thought I was paying a debt I'd been accruing for twenty years.*

*Same thing. Different way of explaining it."*

He picks up what he was working on and continues.

*"Keep talking. Tell me what else you've been reading."*

---

## The Assessment

For the next two hours, the Veteran lays out their operational picture. Burke listens, asks occasional questions that redirect without correcting, and doesn't volunteer his own analysis until the Veteran finishes.

What he is doing, the Veteran realizes midway through, is exactly what he's doing — building an assessment of their assessment. He's listening to what they noticed to understand how they see.

When the Veteran finishes:

*"You're missing three things.*

*One: The Compact checkpoint commander on 16th is a Pragmatist who wants to be a Hardliner. Not there yet — he's still running consequence calculations before he acts. Watch when that shifts. It will shift, and probably not because something changed his mind. It will shift because his evaluation metrics changed. Survival under the current leadership rewards the wrong instincts.*

*Two: The Holdout at the school is three months from a leadership crisis. The community leader has standing. She does not have a succession. If something happens to her — or if the Compact offers her something good enough that she takes it — that community collapses to its second tier, and the second tier is not ready. I have a recommendation if you want it. You don't have to take it.*

*Three: You didn't mention the Coherent site at the eastern edge of your operating area. Either you didn't notice it, which I doubt, or you noticed it and decided it wasn't relevant to what you're telling me, which means you're editing.*

*You shouldn't edit with me. I already know about the site. I want to know what you made of it."*

The Veteran answers. Burke listens to that too.

*"Right,"* he says when they finish. *"That's the right read. Most people don't make that read."*

A pause.

*"The ones who do are usually either Signal or they came up the way you came up. Both are rare. Signal is still treating it as a research problem. You're treating it as an operational environment. That's different.*

*What are you going to do about it?"*

---

## The Difference Between Them

The Veteran, at some point in the first extended conversation, identifies what Burke is doing and says so.

*"You've been building a network. Not a faction — a network. People across faction lines, positioned where they need to be, fed information when they need it."*

Burke doesn't confirm or deny.

*"I've been doing a version of the same thing."*

*"Yes,"* Burke says. *"You have. The difference is scale and duration. You've been at it for weeks. I've been at it for twenty years and then post-Cascade on top of that.*

*The other difference: you came home to it. I've always been here. My baseline is this place. You're applying a framework you built somewhere else, in conditions you were trained to leave.*

*That's not a criticism. The framework is correct. The application is going to require adjustment because you're not leaving.*

*Here's the question I want you to sit with. You know the handover model — you build capacity, you prepare to leave, the thing you built stands after you go. That works when you're the outsider. When you're the person who lives here, what is the handover model? Who are you building for? And what does 'stands after you go' mean when *you* don't go?"*

The Veteran doesn't answer immediately.

Burke nods.

*"Good. That should take a while."*

---

## What Burke Gives Them

Not a mission. Not a faction introduction. Not resources.

Information. Specifically: the two pieces of the operational picture the Veteran has been building that they couldn't reach from the ground — the Compact's internal power balance at the regional command level, and the correlation between the Compact's Project Tether activity and the Corrupted behavior patterns they've been seeing in the eastern zone.

He delivers both pieces without editorializing. He explains the mechanism of Project Tether at a level of detail the Veteran immediately recognizes as operational intelligence, not field observation. He watched it happen. He followed it through the Compact's internal communications through sources he doesn't name.

*"I'm telling you this because you've built the right model and you're going to run into this before long, and I'd rather you hit it with the correct picture than build the wrong one. The Compact didn't create the Corrupted by accident. They created them by doing something they understood and were willing to do anyway. What you do with that knowledge is your business.*

*I have a recommendation there too, if you want it. Same offer."*

---

## The Ongoing Relationship

Burke's property becomes what his fire is for everyone who matters: a place the Veteran can return to when the assessment needs recalibration. Not often. When it matters.

He doesn't reach out to them. They reach out to him — or they show up, which he accepts. He is always there. He is always working on something. The conversation picks up where it left off, weeks or months later, without preamble.

He tracks their Record without the AR overlay and without being asked. The Veteran notices, after a few visits, that he already knows what they've been doing. Not from the Signal and not from the Warden network — from the Holdout leaders and Compact contacts and Separated traders who make up his own network, all of whom have been noticing the same person in their operational environments.

*"You're visible,"* he tells them on the third visit. *"Not in a way that's a problem yet. But you have a pattern, and patterns are legible to people who read patterns. That's useful — you want people who need what you can do to know where to find you. It's also a vulnerability. I want to show you how to run the same operations with a smaller footprint when you need to."*

It is the closest thing he does to training. It is also, in retrospect, when the Veteran understands that Burke has been thinking about how they fit into the larger network since the first conversation.

---

## What This Scene Does

**For the Veteran arc:** Confirms the systems-reading capability as specific and legible — someone else has it, can name it, and has been doing it longer. Gives the Veteran a peer reference point they didn't have. The ODA instinct is not just useful in this environment; it is the correct framework, applied correctly.

**For Burke's arc:** The Veteran is one of the specific people Burke has been identifying and positioning. Not the only one — there is a network, and the player is a node in it, not the center. But the Veteran is a specific kind of node: someone who can read the environment *and* build inside it, which is rare enough that Burke notes it explicitly.

**For the main story:** Burke's Project Tether briefing, given here, accelerates the Veteran's understanding of the Compact's internal dysfunction before it becomes a main story revelation. They hit Act 2's Compact questline with more context than other backgrounds.

**For the relationship:** This is not a warm friendship. It is professional recognition between two people who operate the same way — and the acknowledgment that in a post-Cascade world, finding someone who uses the same model is valuable enough to maintain carefully. Burke has a word for what the Veteran is, and he said it out loud, and the Veteran knows it's specific.

*Responsible.*

He doesn't use the word in this first conversation. He will, later.
