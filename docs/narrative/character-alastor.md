# Alastor — Character Document

*"I've been in rooms where the wrong information killed people. I've been in rooms where the right information killed more. The information isn't the variable. What you do with it is."*

---

## Who He Is

No last name offered. No institutional affiliation acknowledged. The story of how he ended up here changes depending on who's asking and what they've already proven they can handle.

Former intelligence. The specific branch doesn't matter — what matters is the specific skill set: reading people, managing assets, running operations nobody officially knows about, and the particular clarity of a man who spent years understanding how systems work from the inside and came out the other side with no illusions about what systems are for.

The agency burned him or he burned the agency. Both versions are probably true in different ways. What he walked away with: the skills, the contacts, the information, and zero institutional obligation to do anything with any of it in any particular direction.

He ended up in the Signal because the Signal is the only place in the post-Cascade world where knowing everything and answering to nobody is a feature rather than a problem.

---

## The Bond/Silva Register

Not the charming Bond — the burned one. Silva from Skyfall: the intelligence intact, the loyalty to the institution dissolved, the skills sharper because there's no handler telling him what he's allowed to do with them. The charm is real and it is a tool and he has never stopped using it.

The most socially capable person in the Signal. The one who manages first contact, reads new people, decides who gets access to what and in what order. He learned human behavior the way you learn a foreign language — through immersion in environments where getting it wrong had consequences. He doesn't get it wrong.

He is also the one you'd least want to trust with something you couldn't afford to lose. Not because he's disloyal — because his loyalty is to the information and the network and the outcome, not to any individual in it. If losing you serves the larger purpose he will make that calculation and live with it.

He has made that calculation before. He lives with it fine.

---

## First Contact

Alastor manages first contact for the Signal. This is by design — he is the best at reading people quickly and the best at presenting the Signal as something the right person would want to engage with.

The player meets him before they know what they're meeting. A conversation that seems incidental. Someone who knows slightly too much about their recent operations. Charming, specific, unhurried. The assessment is running the whole time.

*"You've been busy. The Coherent site east of the corridor — you were there twice last month. Most people go once and decide they've seen enough. What were you looking for the second time?"*

He already knows the answer. He wants to know if the player does.

---

## What He Knows

He has contacts across every faction. Some of them don't know they're contacts. Some of them know and have decided the exchange is worth it. He has information about the Compact's classified programs that didn't come from Signal research — it came from the people inside the programs who needed someone to tell.

He is the Signal's most dangerous member in the field because he doesn't need the Signal's infrastructure. He could operate entirely alone and has. The network makes him more effective. It does not make him dependent.

---

## The Thing to Watch

He will tell the player things that are true and useful and that serve a purpose he hasn't disclosed. This is not deception — the information is genuinely true and genuinely useful. The undisclosed purpose is also real. These are not in conflict in Alastor's framework.

The player who pays attention will eventually identify the gap between what Alastor shares and what Alastor knows. The question is what they do with that identification. Push on it and Alastor respects them more. Use it against him and the relationship changes in a specific and permanent way.

---

## Voice

Unhurried. Precise word choice — the habit of a man who spent years in environments where imprecision had consequences. Dark humor delivered so dry it takes a moment to land. The smile arrives before the joke, which is disorienting.

*"The Compact has a file on me. Seventeen pages. I've read it. They got some things right. The things they got wrong are the things I needed them to get wrong."*

*"Trust is a transaction. I'm not cynical about that — I'm clear about it. You know what I want, I know what you want, we figure out if the exchange works. That's cleaner than pretending it's something else."*

*"I've been watching your record for three months. You make interesting decisions under pressure. Most people make predictable ones."*
