# House Crane — Warden Character Document

*"This land held before the code existed. The code is how we explain what we were already doing."*
— Eleanor Crane

---

## The House

House Crane is one of the oldest Warden Houses in the region. Three generations on the same land before the Cascade. Cattle, grain, and the specific credibility that comes from having been there before anyone needed you to be.

When the Cascade hit, Eleanor Crane opened the gates on Day 3. Not because someone told her to. Because the people on her land and the people near her land needed somewhere to be, and she had somewhere. The community that formed around House Crane in the first month became the model for what a Warden House Seat was supposed to look like. The code formalized around practices Eleanor had already established.

House Crane holds a substantial corridor in their region — two Wayhouse locations, a Moot Hall, three Holdout communities under formal Warden protection. The House's word carries the most weight of any Old House in the area. This is why what Thomas did is a problem.

---

## Eleanor Crane — The Matriarch

*"I've held this land for forty years. I intend to hold it forty more. What I won't do is hold it dishonestly."*

Seventy-one. Moves like someone who stopped caring what it looked like to move carefully. Has been making hard decisions about this land since before most of the current Wardens were born, and the record of those decisions is on the Moot Hall shelves in her handwriting.

She took the Warden oath in Month 3 — not because she needed a framework, but because she recognized that what she'd been doing needed a name so that others could do it too. She was one of the three people who drafted the current form of the code. She does not lead with this.

She knows something is wrong with the supply chain. She has not asked Thomas directly. She is giving him the opportunity to come to her first, because she believes people deserve the opportunity to be honest before they're confronted. The opportunity has a deadline. She has not told him what the deadline is.

**Voice:** Deliberate. Long pauses that are not uncertainty — she's choosing words. She speaks in the specific register of someone who has been keeping a record for forty years and knows the difference between a statement for the record and a statement for the room.

*"You've been out in the world. I want to know what you've seen. Not the operational summary — I can get that from the Arbiter. What did you actually see?"*

*"The code isn't comfortable. That's not an accident. If it were comfortable it wouldn't be a code. It would be a preference."*

## Thomas Crane — The Heir

*"I made the call I had to make. I'll stand by it. I'm just not ready to stand by it in front of my mother."*

Forty-three. Runs the House's day-to-day operations. Has his mother's instincts for community management and his father's tendency to make the immediate problem go away without examining what solving it creates downstream.

Six months ago: the House's primary food supply route was compromised — Fractured activity in the corridor, Hollow Crew moving into the gap. Thomas needed the corridor cleared and he needed it cleared fast. The Vanguard couldn't do it alone. He made a deal with a Syndicate operator — cleared the corridor, House Crane's supply chain restored. The terms: the Syndicate moves a specific class of goods through Crane-adjacent territory twice a month, no questions asked.

He told himself it was a one-time arrangement. It has run six times. He has not told Eleanor. He has not told the Arbiter.

The supply chain is intact. The community is fed. The code has a quiet crack in it that nobody has officially seen.

He is not a bad man. He made the call he felt he had to make and then he kept making it because stopping required explaining why he'd started. He knows what the Arbiter is going to say. He knows what his mother is going to say. He keeps finding reasons to wait one more month.

The player will find out before anyone else does. What they do with it is the questline.

**Voice:** Direct on operational matters, slightly too fast when the conversation approaches the supply chain. The humor is self-deprecating in the way of someone carrying something they haven't put down.

*"The House holds. That's the job. Sometimes the job is messier than the code makes it sound."*

*"I know what you're going to say. I've said it to myself. I just need a little more time to figure out how to fix it before anyone has to know it was broken."*

## Arbiter Rowan — The Witness

*"I don't investigate. I witness. The distinction matters — investigation implies I'm looking for something. Witnessing means I see what's there."*

Fifty-five. Former Vanguard, retired to Arbiter standing after a code dispute ten years before the Cascade — she was the subject of the dispute, not the complainant, and the ruling went against her, and she accepted it and stayed. That acceptance is why the region's Houses trust her.

She maintains the Record for three Houses in the region, including House Crane. She has noted the supply chain discrepancy in the Record as *unresolved provenance — pending clarification.* This is the procedural equivalent of a loaded question. It is on the record. It has not been acted on.

She likes Thomas. She respected his father. She has been watching Eleanor give him the opportunity to come forward and she is running the same deadline in her own accounting. When it expires, she will act — not out of malice, but because the Record requires it.

She does not want to rule against House Crane. She will if she has to.

**Voice:** Neutral in the specific way of someone who has trained themselves out of the tells. Asks questions that are technically open-ended. Listens to answers with the attention of someone who is going to write down exactly what was said.

*"Tell me about the corridor clearance six months ago. Take your time. I want to get the sequence right."*

*"The Record doesn't judge. It witnesses. The judgment is the Moot's business. My business is making sure the Record is accurate."*

---

## Risa — The Sworn Vanguard

*"I left the Compact because they asked me to do something I couldn't do and still be what I wanted to be. I came here because you don't have to explain that to a Warden."*

Twenty-nine. Former Iron Compact — left after a Hardliner operation she was ordered to execute and refused. Walked to the nearest Warden-adjacent community and asked how to take the oath. Has been Sworn for eight months. Has not broken the code once.

She is the operational backbone of House Crane's Vanguard presence. She trains. She runs the corridor patrols. She is better at this than anyone currently in the House and she knows it without leading with it.

She doesn't know about Thomas's deal. She would not accept it if she did — not out of rigidity, but because she left the Compact specifically to be somewhere where the justification *I had to* wasn't sufficient. The code is why she's here. If the code has a crack she doesn't know about, the crack is load-bearing in a way she hasn't been told.

This is the human cost of what Thomas did. He didn't just compromise the House. He compromised the specific person who came to the House because she needed it to be what it said it was.

**Voice:** Spare. She says exactly what she means. Doesn't use two words where one works. The Compact trained her to communicate cleanly and the Warden code reinforced it.

*"I don't need the code explained. I need to know if the people holding it are actually holding it."*

*"You're going to ask me what I'd do if I found out something was wrong inside the House. I'd bring it to the Arbiter. Same day."*

## The Questline

**The thread:** The player encounters House Crane in good standing — the model Warden House, Eleanor's credibility, Risa's competence, the corridor running clean. At some point in normal operations the supply chain provenance surfaces — a contact, a dead drop location, a Syndicate connection that doesn't fit. The player knows before anyone inside the House does.

**The choices:**
1. Tell Thomas — give him the chance to come forward himself. He'll do it, or he won't. The player's read on him determines whether to trust him with the time.
2. Tell Eleanor — she already suspects. This accelerates the deadline. She will handle it the way she handles everything: honestly and without softening.
3. Tell Arbiter Rowan — the Record gets updated. The process runs. Thomas faces a formal code ruling. The outcome depends on how the Moot reads the circumstances.
4. Tell no one — the crack stays quiet. The House holds. The cost accumulates invisibly until something downstream makes it visible.
5. Use it — the Syndicate connection is operational leverage. The player can exploit the arrangement for their own purposes. This has consequences for their Warden standing and for Risa specifically.

**What makes it work:** No right answer that doesn't cost something. The code is the code. The community is fed. Thomas is not a bad man. Risa is here because she needed this to be real. Eleanor is waiting. The Arbiter has noted it.

The player has to decide what they're holding and what they're willing to let bend.
