# Audio Direction — Announcement Trailer
*30-second spot. Signal-to-coherence. No score until Act 3.*

---

## Philosophy

The audio mirrors the visual. Both channels — voice and image — are coming online from degraded state to coherence. They drop out and return independently. When they sync at 0:20, that's the emotional payoff. Audio editing must preserve that independence.

**No score until Act 3.** Music before the voice is coherent is a crutch. Let the silence and the degraded voice do the work.

---

## Act 1 — 0:00–0:10 | DARK

**What's playing:** Nothing except the assistant's voice — degraded, background-positioned, below conversational level.

**Voice treatment:**
- Source: the final Act 3 monologue, processing reversed into garble
- Effect chain: pitch shift cycling (±2–4 semitones), buffer repeat, formant scramble — the rhythm and cadence of speech is present, syllables are not
- Positioning: not centered. Slight left or right. Feels like heard-through-a-wall, or a degraded radio signal
- Level: -12 to -15dB relative to where the clear voice will land in Act 3. Background, not foreground.

**Silence is part of the mix.** The garbled voice should drop out completely for 1–2 seconds around 0:05–0:07. The silence is where the first visual glimpse lands. Let them coincide.

**No music. No ambient texture. No room tone.** The black has no atmosphere. It has a signal.

---

## Act 2 — 0:10–0:20 | GLIMPSES

**What's playing:** Voice improving. Still processed but phonemes are resolving into words. Words arriving in wrong order.

**Voice treatment:**
- Reduce pitch cycling — occasional stutter/buffer repeat remains, but the formant scramble is gone
- Positioning: moving toward center. Not there yet.
- Level: rising toward -6dB relative to Act 3 clear voice
- Occasional clean word breaking through the processing — "world," "home," "people" — before the processing reasserts

**Sample lines (wrong-order syntax):**
- *"Tell them — world. Did not end."*
- *"Work — people — doing it."*
- *"Come home. First. We — go from there."*

**Ambient:** At around 0:15, barely audible room tone begins — the lamp's electrical hum, or faint mechanical presence. Not music. Physical space establishing itself. Low pass filtered, -20dB. The viewer doesn't consciously hear it but the black starts to feel *occupied*.

**No music yet.**

---

## Act 3 — 0:20–0:30 | COHERENT

**What's playing:** Voice clear. No processing. The signal is gone. Underscore begins.

**Voice treatment:**
- Dry. No reverb tail, or minimal room reverb only (small room, warm — consistent with the kitchen interior). The clarity is the contrast with everything before.
- Centered, foreground. This is where he is.
- Measured delivery. Not warm initially — deliberate, choosing words. Warmth arrives by the second sentence.

**Monologue:**
> *"You want to know what to tell them? After everything?"*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *"Tell them the world didn't end. Tell them it got complicated. Tell them there's work to do, and the people doing it are more interesting than the ones who gave up."*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *"Tell them to come home first. We'll go from there."*

**Underscore — enters at 0:20, under the clear voice:**

| Property | Spec |
|----------|------|
| Character | Single instrument — not a score, not a theme. A texture. Piano or prepared piano, or muted string. |
| Tonality | Warm minor. Not melancholy — reflective. Something that has been waiting a long time. |
| Level | -18dB under voice at entry. Rises to -12dB by the time the voice finishes. Never competes. |
| Duration | Enters at 0:20, holds through title card, fades on cut to black. Does not swell. |
| Rule | No percussion. No orchestral build. One voice has already been doing all the work — the music should feel like it's been there the whole time and we just heard it. |

**The eye flip (0:28–0:29):**
No audio cue. No sting, no mechanical click. The instant red-flip is visual only. Silence after the last word, through the title card. The viewer fills in what they feel.

---

## Title Card — 0:28–0:30

Silence. The underscore fades under the title card. Nothing else.

The last thing the viewer hears is the assistant's voice. The last thing they see is the name of the game. No audio bridge.

---

## Sync Notes

- Voice and visual drops in Acts 1–2 should not be rhythmically regular — stagger them so they feel like independent signal channels, not editing choices
- The moment of sync at 0:20 should feel like relief — voice clarity and visual hold arriving close together but not exactly simultaneously (voice a half-beat before image)
- The underscore entering at 0:20 should feel like it was already playing and we only now hear it — fade-in from silence over 2–4 seconds, not a deliberate entry

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a hype trailer. No epic score, no bass drop, no tension-building percussion
- Not horror audio. The garble in Act 1 is a degraded signal — not a monster, not a haunting. The processing choices should reference *radio static and communication failure*, not distortion-as-threat
- Not emotional manipulation. The underscore exists to give the voice somewhere to land, not to tell the viewer how to feel about what they're hearing
