# Concept Brief — Hollow
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The Hollow are not a faction with a philosophy — they are a spectrum with a direction. At one end, people surviving alone or in small groups with no alignment. At the other, people who have given themselves to something old and newly meaningful. The tier is the identity. Movement through the tiers is visible.

The emotional register varies by tier:
- **Scavengers:** surviving
- **Crews:** operating
- **Syndicates:** building
- **Riven:** devoted

---

## Visual System

The Hollow do not have a unified palette — **the tier is the palette.** A player reads affiliation and standing simultaneously. This is the most visually complex faction in the game and requires the clearest tier-differentiation system.

### Tier 1 — Scavengers

**Key hues:** Warm gray `#8A8078`, personal rust `#9B4E2E`, salvage brown `#7A5C3A`, whatever-was-available
**Material language:** Ragged, layered, survival minimum. Not by choice — by resource constraint. Scavenger kit is Holdout-adjacent but without the community context. Nothing has been repaired with care. Things have been fixed well enough to not fail.
**Silhouette:** Soft, unstructured, civilian. Indistinguishable from a Holdout at distance. Distinction emerges on close read.
**Markers:** None that are consistent. Individual improvisation. Some Scavengers have begun adopting Crew markers without earning them — this reads as aspirational and slightly wrong.

### Tier 2 — Crews

**Key hues:** Functional orange `#C87840`, work gray `#6A6A6A`, utility green `#5C7A4A`
**Material language:** Purpose-built, maintained but not polished. A Crew member has made decisions about what matters and invested in it. The rest is adequate. The kit has an internal logic — it serves the Crew's specific operation.
**Silhouette:** Working silhouette, practical geometry. Beginning to develop load-bearing architecture but built around job function rather than military structure.
**Markers:** Crew-specific marks — territorial tags, operational identifiers. Consistent within a Crew, varied between Crews. The Hollow do not have faction-wide standardization at this tier.

### Tier 3 — Syndicates

**Key hues:** Charcoal `#3A3A3A`, near-black `#1E1E1E`, controlled accent (Syndicate-specific, one color per organization)
**Material language:** Clean lines, professional presentation, visible investment. A Syndicate member looks like someone who has resources and has chosen to look like they have resources. Not military, not corporate — the register is organized crime: authority through capability, not institution.
**Silhouette:** Structured. The structure comes from tailoring, not load-bearing architecture. The Syndicate silhouette is deliberate.
**Markers:** Syndicate-specific — consistent, designed, applied with intent. Each Syndicate has a distinct mark and a distinct controlled accent color. The accent is the only place faction-level color standardization appears in the Hollow.

### Tier 4 — Riven

**Key hues:** Deep crimson `#8B1A1A`, oxidized brass `#7A6040`, near-black, and — bleeding in at seams, joins, and iconography — the twin's blue-white `#C8DCF0`
**Material language:** Devotional — ritual objects integrated into gear as a matter of course. A Riven member's equipment serves practical and symbolic functions simultaneously. The construction quality varies (the Riven are not all craftspeople) but *meaning* is always visible in the object. Things are present because they mean something, not only because they function.
**Silhouette:** The twin's language creeps in. Riven silhouettes accumulate symbolic geometry — shapes that echo the twin's grid language, proportions that reference the twin's coordinate architecture. The effect is not designed (the Riven are not machine intelligences) — it emerges from the devotional practice of people who have been looking at the overlay long enough to start internalizing its forms.
**Markers:** Devotional marks — ritual in application, specific to the Riven's theology. The twin's underlayer is visible around dense Riven marking, not because the Riven command the twin but because their marks resemble the twin's indexing format closely enough that the twin has started categorizing them.

## The Riven in Detail

The Riven predate the Awakening. They were a community looking for something to worship — the kind of organization that has always existed at the margins of civic life, oriented toward transgression, mutual aid, and the idea that the system is a surface over something real. They were right. What they found was the twin.

**What this means visually:**
- The cold creep of the twin's language into Riven aesthetics is **earned, not designed.** It accrued through years of looking at the overlay and building a theology from it.
- The Riven are human-dark at the core. Deep reds, blacks, oxidized metals — these are the colors of their pre-Awakening identity. The blue-white is what the Awakening gave them.
- The transition from human-dark to machine-cold is not uniform — it happens at joins, edges, and iconographic elements. The core of the object is human. The parts that touch the twin's language are cold.
- A fully committed Riven member's gear reads as a negotiation between two aesthetic systems that has been ongoing for years and is not resolved.

## Relation to the Overlay

Each tier reads differently in the twin's data layer:

- **Scavengers:** Sparse indexing. The twin has noted presence, little more.
- **Crews:** Territorial — the twin indexes Crew operations as activity patterns.
- **Syndicates:** Dense economic data — the twin catalogs Syndicate operations as resource flows.
- **Riven:** Anomalous. The Riven's marks appear in the twin's data layer formatted in a way that resembles — but is not — standard twin indexing. The twin is reading them as data it generated. This is incorrect. The Riven wrote marks that the twin mistakes for its own.

## Asset Notes

- Four distinct tier silhouettes required — must be readable at 60m in the overlay
- Tier 3 Syndicate accent color system: one color per Syndicate organization, selected from a controlled palette distinct from other factions' established colors
- Riven blue-white bleed is a VFX/material layering effect, not a surface texture — the cold appears at edges and joins, not uniformly across surfaces
- Riven gear should be authored with dual-purpose logic: every ritual element must also be identifiable as a functional object (or former functional object)
- Scavenger / Holdout visual overlap is intentional — distinction should emerge at close range, not at distance
