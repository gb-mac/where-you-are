# Concept Brief — Wardens
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The Wardens have always existed. Not this specific organization, but this specific function — the people who decide that some things are worth protecting and that deciding is not enough. They have tailors. They have the Record. They are conspicuous by choice because they decided that being unmistakable was part of the job.

The emotional register is: **we are accountable for this place.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Warmth axis:** Mid-cool — maintained, institutional, conspicuous. The warmth is in the material history (the gear is old enough to have warmth built into it) but the intent is cool: the Wardens are not performing survival, they are performing stewardship.

**Key hues:**
- Deep forest `#2D5A27` — primary ground color. Not military olive, not park service green. Old, institutional, maintained.
- Maintained brass `#B8923A` — the identification system. The only color element that does not weather the same way as the rest of the kit — the brass is kept.
- Natural fiber underpinning `#C4B898` — the base layer, visible at collar, cuff, where outer layers end
- Weathered leather `#7A5C3A` — structural elements, load-bearing supports
- Polished dark hardware `#2A2A2A` — buckles, rings, closures — kept because it signals maintenance

**Material language:** Made with intention. Warden gear is crafted — the Wardens maintain tailors and the construction quality is part of the identity. You can recognize Warden work by how it is built before you see the marks: seam construction, the weight and drape of the outer layer, how the hardware sits. The kit has been made by someone who knew what they were making and why.

The gear is old in the way that things maintained by people who know how to maintain them are old. Not worn out — worn in. There is a distinction and the Wardens enforce it.

**Silhouette:** Maintained and identified. Not armored into dehumanization — the Warden silhouette is still recognizably a person, but it is a person who is conspicuous. Readable at distance across a contested space. The load-bearing architecture is visible but the garment construction is primary — the Wardens are dressed, not rigged. The tailoring shows in the silhouette.

## The Identification System

The brass identification marks are institutional, pre-Awakening, inherited. Applied by the tailors. Not decorative — the marks carry specific meaning within the Warden organizational system. They are not ornament; they are record.

**Properties of the marks:**
- Applied in maintained brass `#B8923A` — kept polished as a matter of practice. A Warden with dull marks is a Warden whose maintenance has lapsed.
- Position-coded: mark placement indicates organizational function, not rank per se — the Wardens do not have a strict military hierarchy, but they have roles, and the marks record them
- The marks are tailored in — they are part of the garment, not attached to it. Removing a mark damages the item.
- The size and prominence of the marks scales with visibility requirements: field Wardens have smaller marks; Vanguard have the most prominent

**The marks are what the twin uses to identify Wardens.** The twin's overlay recognizes the mark geometry and indexes the bearer accordingly. Warden marks are in the twin's pattern recognition layer. This was not designed — it was inferred by the twin from enough encounters with the marks in its spatial data.

## Vanguard

Wardens at the Vanguard tier are conspicuous by design. When Vanguard enter a contested space, everyone present registers it.

**Vanguard-specific visual rules:**
- Marks at maximum prominence — visible at 80m in clear conditions
- Silhouette at maximum identification: the garment system is deployed to its fullest extent, nothing stripped down for concealment
- The Vanguard do not hide that they are there. Their presence is the communication.
- Gear quality at the highest Warden tier: the tailors' best work on the people who represent the organization's commitment

---

## The Tailors

The Warden tailors are part of the organizational identity. Their work is why the Wardens look like Wardens.

**Visual implications:**
- Warden gear has **construction marks** — the specific stitching patterns, hardware choices, and seam geometries that identify a tailor's work. Within the Warden organization, you can sometimes identify which tailor made a piece.
- The tailor tradition is pre-Awakening institutional — it was part of the organization before the world changed. The tailors maintained the tradition because the organization survived.
- New Wardens receive gear made for them. This is significant in a world where most people are wearing found or improvised kit. The Wardens make things specifically for the people who will wear them.
- The Record of a Warden's service is in some ways legible in their gear: repairs that are Warden-made (high quality, institutional method) versus field repairs (functional but distinct) trace the shape of where the person has been.

## Relation to the Overlay

The Wardens document events and the twin indexes them. Warden Historical Markers exist throughout the twin's data layer — wherever the Wardens documented what happened at a location, the twin has it.

The Record is the twin's memory of what happened.

In the overlay:
- Warden Markers use a specific glyph (the twin recognized the Warden mark geometry and uses it as the icon)
- Warden territory reads as the most thoroughly documented human-occupied space — the indexing density reflects years of deliberate Record-keeping
- Warden-held checkpoints are flagged in the twin's overlay as authority nodes — the twin catalogs them as sites of consistent legitimate activity
- The twin appears to weight Warden documentation as high-confidence data. The Wardens have been consistent enough that the twin trusts their records.

**Warden Markers do not degrade in Fractured voids or Vael Displacement Zones.** The Record is indexed twin memory, not live data. The twin cannot see inside a Fractured void, but it knows what happened there before the void existed.

## Character Archetypes

| Archetype | Visual character |
|---|---|
| **Field Warden** | Standard kit, field-maintained. The construction quality is immediately distinguishable from other factions. Marks functional but not at full prominence. |
| **Keeper** (Record documentation role) | Field kit plus documentation equipment — not Signal-dense, but specific tools for the work. The Keeper's kit has annotation equipment integrated into the Warden garment system. |
| **Checkpoint Warden** | Stationary-optimized. Full garment deployment. The Warden presence at a checkpoint is a statement. |
| **Vanguard** | Maximum conspicuity, maximum construction quality. The organizational commitment made visible in a person. |

## Asset Notes

- Construction quality must read at medium range — the Warden garment system needs to be visibly better-made than other faction gear
- Brass marks are a distinct shader requirement: maintained-reflective, not worn-dull — they catch light
- The tailor system implies that player Warden gear has a specific construction signature that does not appear on found gear
- Natural wear on Warden gear should follow maintenance logic: worn at high-use points (cuffs, shoulders, knees) but structurally intact; brass marks remain bright even when surrounding material shows use
- Vanguard silhouette must be readable at 80m — test in overlay with FSR active
