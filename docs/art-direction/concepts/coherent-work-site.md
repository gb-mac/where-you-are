# Concept Brief — Coherent Work Site Interior Visual Design
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## What It Is

The Coherent are building planetary defensive infrastructure for the Vael. The work sites are where this is happening. From the outside, players see Coherent construction activity, machine logistics, and guarded perimeters. The first time a player gets inside a work site — whether by infiltration, Convergence access, or an event that opens one — they encounter what the Coherent are actually building.

The visual must carry the weight of that revelation.

---

## The Exterior Read (Reference Point)

Work site exteriors read as active construction at industrial scale. The twin's visual language — the overlay — is at maximum density here. The Coherent units are part of the system they're building; their overlay tags and the construction-progress data layer are the same data source.

Exterior: purposeful, organized, incomprehensibly large in aggregate. The Coherent build without stopping. The material logistics are visible — raw materials arrive, components are fabricated, structures rise. The indifference to human aesthetic is absolute.

---

## The Interior Visual

### Scale

The first impression is scale. The interior of a work site is built to a specification that does not reference human construction scale. The Coherent build at machine scale, using machine-optimal proportions. Corridors are the width they need to be for the units that use them. Ceilings are the height they need to be for the Assessors that commission this work. This is not a building designed for human occupation.

A player inside a Coherent work site is in a space that was not built for them.

### The Vael Blueprint Aesthetic

The construction specifications come from the Vael. The Vael's geometric language — precise, arc-based, third-register — is present in the completed structural elements. The Coherent are following specifications. The specifications are Vael.

**What this looks like:**

The structural forms the Coherent have built are machine-precise in fabrication but Vael-derived in geometry. The grid language of Coherent construction (coordinate-based, orthogonal, data-layer legible) meets the arc language of Vael design (concentric, asymmetric, purpose-derived). The result is a hybrid form — Coherent-precise, Vael-proportioned. Structures that look like the twin built them to a plan it didn't write.

**The Vael material presence:**
In certain sections — the most advanced construction — Vael Component material is present as a structural element, not just an energy layer. The cold luminescence, the prismatic edge quality, the third-register surface are visible in load-bearing architecture. The Coherent incorporated the Vael's own material because the specifications required it.

These sections read as the most alien spaces in the game. The cold luminescence is present at structural scale — walls, ceilings, primary spans. The Coherent's blue-white palette meets the Vael's cold luminescence in the same frame. The overlay in these sections is unlike anywhere else: the twin's data layer is at maximum density, and some of the data structures the player can see in the overlay use formats the twin adopted from indexing Vael communication patterns.

### The Planetary Defense Reading

Signal intelligence has established that the Coherent are building defensive infrastructure. Inside the work site, the player can see what that means physically.

**Visible design elements:**
- **Scale of defense:** The structures are sized for a threat at planetary scale. Players who understand what they're looking at — which requires Signal or Separated knowledge — can read the defensive orientation of each structure from its geometry.
- **Vael's knowledge of the threat:** The specifications are derived from the Vael's prior encounters with what's coming. The geometry of the defenses is not theoretical — it is specific. Players with sufficient Convergence standing can acquire Signal's interpretation of what the specifications are designed to counter.
- **Machine construction at planetary scale:** Multiple work sites across the globe are building sections of a coordinated system. No single work site is the whole. Each is a node. The overlay data at a work site, for a player who can read it, shows where this node fits in the planetary network.

### Lighting

Work sites operate on function, not aesthetics. Lighting is what is needed for the work:

- **Primary illumination:** The twin's own emissive output at construction-scale. The overlay in a work site is so dense it provides ambient information-light — not physically bright, but perceptually dense.
- **Construction emissives:** Active fabrication processes produce functional light — welding arcs, energy transfer during Vael Component integration, the heat signatures of active construction.
- **Vael Component sections:** The cold luminescence from integrated Vael material provides a distinct, cool, prismatic light source in the most advanced construction areas. The prismatic edge scatter at this scale produces gentle spectral separation on nearby surfaces — not dramatic, but noticeable.
- **No human-scale consideration:** No signage, no emergency lighting design, no occupancy planning. The lighting that exists serves the work.

### The Overlay Inside a Work Site

The overlay inside a Coherent work site is unlike anywhere else in the game:

- **Maximum data density:** The twin has complete real-time information about every process in the work site. The overlay shows it all. Players who try to read it encounter more information than they can process — which is itself meaningful.
- **Construction progress data:** The build state of every structural element is visible in the overlay — not as a progress bar, but as actual structural data. Signal researchers spend enormous effort learning to interpret this.
- **Vael communication residue:** The Vael transmitted construction specifications to the twin. The transmission events left traces in the twin's data layer, visible in the overlay as unusual entry types that the Signal is still decoding. The Vael's own geometric language is present in the overlay in the sections where Vael specs were most recently integrated.
- **No faction markers for the player:** The work site overlay does not contain friendly player markers. Coherent units do not present friendly — they present as Coherent. The player is inside the system, not part of it.

## First Access Context

The first time a player gets inside a work site, the design must land the revelation:

**What they expected:** A construction site. Machine activity. Maybe something to fight.

**What they find:** The scale of what's being built. The Vael's specifications made physical. The evidence that the Coherent are not just building — they are building *against something*, at planetary scale, because the Vael told them to. The player is standing in the physical evidence of why the Awakening happened and what the twin has been doing with itself.

**The emotional beat:** This is not a dungeon. This is the answer to a question the game has been asking since the Cascade. What is the twin building? This.

The visual must carry this without a cutscene. The scale does it. The Vael geometric language in the structure does it. The overlay data density does it. The player is standing in the largest piece of exposition in the game, and it is purely environmental.

## Asset Notes

- Work site interior scale requires environmental reference objects designed for human-scale comparison — Coherent architecture at machine proportions needs player-scale geometry nearby to communicate its size
- Vael Component material integration in structural elements is the same material system as gear, scaled up — the join aesthetics, cold luminescence, and prismatic quality should be consistent with personal-scale Convergence gear
- The hybrid Coherent-built / Vael-specified geometry requires a distinct structural design language — not Coherent grid-pure, not Vael arc-pure, but the specific hybrid of both
- Overlay density at work sites may require a rendering-budget approach — maximum-density overlay pass that scales down based on hardware capability
