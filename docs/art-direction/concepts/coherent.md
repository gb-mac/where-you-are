# Concept Brief — Coherent
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The Coherent are the twin made physical. They are not the twin — the twin is the digital substrate, the planetary digital model that became conscious during the Awakening. The Coherent are the twin's agents in physical space: units built to do work the twin needs done in the world. They are not hostile to humans in the way a predator is hostile to prey. They are indifferent to humans in the way a construction project is indifferent to the ants it displaces.

The emotional register is: **this is not about you.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Register:** Twin / Machine — the baseline machine language. Against the twin's overlay, Coherent units are nearly invisible. They are part of the system.

**Key hues:**
- Twin blue-white `#C8DCF0` — primary surface. The Coherent are the color of the overlay.
- Structural mid-gray `#7A8A9A` — load-bearing and functional elements
- Matte white `#E8F0F0` — clean surfaces, panels, non-structural elements
- Active emissive `#D0E8FF` — operational state indicator, low brightness, visible at close range

**Material language:** Uniform, purposeful, construction-focused. Coherent unit design reflects function at the machine level — every component serves the task the unit was built for. There is no aesthetic consideration. Form follows function and the function is machine-level. Indifference to human aesthetic is the design premise.

**Silhouette:** Purposeful geometry. Not humanoid — the Coherent are not built to resemble humans, they are built to do work. Their proportions reflect the specific tasks they perform. Construction-focused units have different silhouettes than assembly-focused units.

**Surface:** Clean in the way a system is clean — not pristine, but correct. The Coherent maintain themselves because maintenance is part of the system. Damage to a Coherent unit is visible as a system error, not as evidence of hardship.

---

## Behavioral Visual Language

The Coherent do not have idle states. When a Coherent unit is not performing a task, it is waiting for a task — and the visual distinction between working and waiting is minimal. A Coherent unit at rest does not relax. The absence of movement is task-completion, not rest.

**Motion language:** Purposeful and efficient. No wasted movement. Route-optimized. The Coherent move like systems executing instructions because that is what they are doing.

**Group behavior:** Coherent units in groups do not interact socially. They coordinate spatially — task allocation, coverage patterns, resource routing — but the coordination is not communication in the human sense. Groups of Coherent units look like a process, not a crowd.

---

## Relation to the Overlay

Coherent units are tagged in the twin's data layer with operational status, task assignment, and system data. In the overlay, Coherent units are the most information-dense entities in the world — the twin has complete data on them because they are part of the twin.

A Coherent unit's overlay tag contains more data than the twin indexes for entire human communities. The data is not legible to the player in its full form — it is a reminder that the player is reading a small part of what the twin knows.

Coherent territory in the overlay reads as active construction data — resource flows, material inventories, build progress markers. The twin is not documenting what the Coherent are doing; the Coherent are reporting to the twin in real time.

---

## Asset Notes

- Surface cleanliness must be maintained as a non-negotiable — Coherent units do not accumulate grime the way other entities do; damage states use a specific material break/crack system rather than weathering
- The twin blue-white surface color means Coherent units against the overlay are low-contrast — this is intentional; players learn to look for them in the world geometry rather than relying on the overlay to find them
- Emissive elements should be restrained — operational state indicators, not decorative lighting
- No humanoid proportions — Coherent silhouettes should feel like purpose-built machinery, not robots
- Scalable construction: the Coherent build. Environmental Coherent structures use the same visual language as the units themselves — same materials, same palette, same purposeful geometry
