# Art Direction — Announcement Trailer
*30-second TV spot. The assistant, coming online.*

---

## Format

**Runtime:** 30 seconds, hard.
**Structure:** Three 10-second acts. Garbled → Yoda/Bumblebee → Coherent.
**Default state:** Black. Everything is earned from black.

---

## Visual Language

**Black is the base.** Not dark grey. Not deep space cinematic. Black. The trailer lives in it. Light intrudes briefly and retreats. What the viewer sees is not a scene — it's evidence of a scene. Glimpses.

**The light is unreliable.** It comes in and cuts out. Not flickering (that's horror) — more like a signal dropping. Present, then absent. Like someone is controlling it and isn't fully in control yet. Each glimpse slightly longer, slightly clearer than the last.

**What the glimpses show:** Interior space — the room — but never fully. A surface. A shape. A cable. The warm amber of the lamp in the background, briefly. Never enough to orient completely. The viewer is assembling something from fragments, the same way the voice is assembling language.

---

## The Three Acts

---

### ACT 1 — 0:00–0:10 | DARK

**Visual:**
Black. Near-total.

One brief glimpse — 1–2 frames at most — of warm amber light in the far background. Gone before the eye registers it fully. Then black again.
Maybe a second glimpse around 0:07, slightly longer. An edge of a surface. A shape. Nothing readable.

The viewer isn't sure they saw anything.

**Audio:**
The assistant's voice, in background — not foreground. Like something heard through a wall, or a recording degrading. Garbled beyond meaning. Phonemes cycling, no words forming. The intelligence is clearly present in the rhythm. The language is not.

*[static / looping fragments / signal cycling]*

No music yet. Just this.

---

### ACT 2 — 0:10–0:20 | GLIMPSES

**Visual:**
The black starts giving. Not continuously — still cutting in and out — but now the glimpses hold for a beat before dropping. Three or four of them across this section.

What they show:
- The room. Interior. Warm. Domestic. Out of focus at edges.
- The lamp in background — amber, distinct, clearly been on a long time.
- A surface with objects (never legible — shape only).
- Near the end of this act: a shape that is *him*. Not clear. Not a reveal. A silhouette, or a partial form. Seated or standing at a workbench. The viewer registers: there is someone here.

**Eyes:** In the last glimpse of this act — 0:18 or 0:19 — two points of light. Red. Low in the frame. Brief. Then black.

**Audio:**
Voice improving. Syntax off. Yoda-cadence, Bumblebee-cadence — words arriving in the wrong order, but words. Each sentence more assembled than the last.

*"Tell them — world. Did not end."*

*"Work — people — doing it."*

*"Come home. First. We — go from there."*

Not clean. Not confident yet. Getting there.

---

### ACT 3 — 0:20–0:30 | COHERENT

**Visual:**
The room holds. Not a full reveal — still intimate, still close — but the black is no longer dominant. The glimpses are longer now, closer to sustained shots. Warm. Real. The lamp is always visible.

The assistant is now partially visible. Not a hero shot. The camera doesn't pull back to give them space — it stays close, observational. What we see: the shape of them. Seated. The workbench behind. Hands, maybe. The eyes.

**The eyes:** Red to green — but not settled. The transition plays out: red holds, flickers, moves toward green, steadies. You think it's over. Then — in the last half-second before cut — red again. Brief. Gone. Black.

Not a malfunction. Not a glitch. A choice, or something like one.

The viewer doesn't know if they're watching someone wake up or something that doesn't want to be understood.

**Audio:**
Voice clear. Measured. The signal is gone. This is who he is.

*"You want to know what to tell them? After everything?"*

Beat.

*"Tell them the world didn't end. Tell them it got complicated. Tell them there's work to do, and the people doing it are more interesting than the ones who gave up."*

Beat.

*"Tell them to come home first. We'll go from there."*

---

### TITLE CARD — 0:28–0:30

Black. Then:

**Where You Are**

Warm off-white on black. Mixed case. Clean humanist sans-serif — something in the Gill Sans / Optima family, medium weight, open spacing. No glow, no animation beyond a clean 0.3s fade in.

Holds. Fades or cuts.

## The Assistant — Design Notes

The glimpses establish him as a presence before the title card. A few constraints for the character design, informed by what the trailer needs:

**Silhouette:** Readable in partial view. The shape of him — seated at a workbench — needs to read as *occupied, competent* from a dark partial frame.

**The eyes:** The only clearly designed feature visible in the trailer. They carry the entire question the trailer is asking — and the trailer refuses to answer it.

Design direction:
- Not glowing orbs — irises, with the depth of a real eye, but with luminance behind them
- Red: desaturated, amber-edged. Not villain-red. Closer to a warning light that's been on so long you stop registering it as danger.
- Green: warm. Not neon, not status-board green. Close to the lamp's temperature — almost comfortable. Almost.
- The transition between them is the thing: it should feel like thought, not circuitry. A decision being made, or reconsidered.

**The last-second flip back to red:** This is the trailer's final image. Green appears settled — then doesn't. One beat of red. Cut to black. Cut to title.

The viewer's read of everything they just watched — the warmth, the voice, the lamp, the words — just became a question. Friend or foe? Waking up, or performing waking up? The trailer does not answer. The game will, slowly, maybe.

**Design affinity — Real Steel (Atom):** The aesthetic reference is Atom specifically — not the villain bots, not the polished ones. Atom is worn, patched, has been through something. The eyes on a face that doesn't otherwise move much carry everything. That's the target: expressiveness living almost entirely in the eyes, on a form that is otherwise still and unreadable. You project onto it. You're not sure if that projection is correct.

## The Room — Revised Direction

The room's warmth is present in the trailer but earned, not given. It appears in glimpses. By the time the viewer sees it clearly in Act 3, they've been waiting 20 seconds for it.

**Palette:** The room is warm amber against deep black surroundings. High contrast. The lamp is the source — everything readable in the frame is readable because of that lamp.

**The lamp:** Small, warm, clearly running independent of anything unreliable. Always on. Its constancy is the counterpoint to everything else in the trailer that's intermittent. It doesn't flicker. It doesn't cut out. While the assistant's voice drops and returns, while the visuals go dark and come back — the lamp is always there when the glimpses show it.

**What NOT to see:** No decay, no damage, no survival staging. The room is maintained. That matters. Whatever the Cascade was, this person kept their space.

---

## Audio/Visual Sync Notes

The cutting in and out of visuals should not be rhythmically regular. Don't cut on the beat if there's a beat. The drops should feel like signal, not editing. Slightly irregular. The voice and the visuals drop independently — they are two channels of the same thing coming back online, not one.

The moment the voice becomes fully coherent at 0:20, the room holds. The two channels sync. That's the payoff.
