# Concept Brief — Covenant
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The Covenant are wrong in a way that is very difficult to locate. Their gear is clean. Their people are composed. Their facilities are operational in ways that shouldn't be possible given the supply environment. The wrongness is not in any single visible thing — it is in the sum of what shouldn't add up but does.

The emotional register is: **how are they this calm?**

---

## Visual Identity

**Warmth axis:** False. Clinical off-white surface that reads as clean and organized, over something the eye cannot see but the twin can.

**Key hues:**
- Clinical off-white `#F5F0E8` — the primary surface. Not pure white. Pure white reads as sterile or technological. This is slightly warm but the warmth is wrong, like something that has been washed too many times.
- Underlayer violet `#7C5C8A` — not visible on the surface. Present in the twin's overlay data layer around Covenant members and structures. The twin reads something in them.
- Matte mid-gray `#9A9A9A` — secondary surface, hardware, structural elements
- Pale bone `#E8E0D0` — aged materials that have been cleaned but not replaced

**Material language:** Pristine, matching, minimal. The Covenant do not improvise. Their gear fits. Their kit is not layered survival additions — it is a consistent system, applied consistently. The question this poses is not answered by looking at the gear. The gear is fine. The gear is better than fine. That is the problem.

**Silhouette:** Composed, unhurried. Clean lines with nothing extraneous. No dangling additions, no improvised attachments. The silhouette is the silhouette of people who have not been forced to adapt, and that is the most unsettling thing about them.

---

## Identity Markers

The Covenant marking system is **minimal surface, deep data.**

- Small geometric mark on the right shoulder — simple, consistent, unremarkable at distance
- The twin's overlay reveals more. Covenant members have dense data annotations in the twin's layer that are not legible as ordinary twin indexing. The information is there; its format is not standard.
- **No rank visible to outside observers.** Covenant hierarchy is not expressed through gear. It is expressed through behavior.

---

## The Underlayer Violet

The violet `#7C5C8A` appears exclusively in the overlay — never on the surface. Rules for its appearance:

- Subtle bloom in the twin's data layer around individual Covenant members — not a marker, a quality of how the data reads
- Covenant structures pulse very faintly with it in the overlay when viewed in the dark
- The player encounters it before they understand what it means
- It does not degrade in Fractured voids or Vael Displacement Zones — the underlayer is not live data, it is something the twin indexed and cannot un-index

**Narrative grounding confirmed** (`factions-humans-depth.md`): The violet is the twin's own classification tag — not spiritual recognition, not an invitation. It is a monitoring entry.

The Covenant's practice of spending extended time near active Coherent work sites causes measurable neurological and electromagnetic changes in long-term practitioners. The twin's distributed sensor arrays, which monitor everything near construction zones with high precision, log anomalous biological signatures — not machine-pattern, not standard human. The twin created a classification for "subject of ongoing biological monitoring." The violet is that classification, rendered visible in the overlay.

**Ascendants specifically** emit a low-level EM resonance that the twin registers as partially coherent with its own signal architecture — not machine-pattern, not noise. Something in between. The twin logs them separately from standard Covenant members. Their overlay entries have additional notations that appear to be observation records.

**The design principle for the violet:** it is a bureaucratic tag, not a halo. It should read as data notation, not spiritual light. The player who understands what it is will find it more unsettling than someone who mistakes it for validation. The Covenant has earned the twin's attention the way an unusual specimen earns a scientist's attention.

**What the violet does NOT indicate:** the twin welcoming the Covenant, the twin communicating with the Covenant, or the Covenant's theology being correct. It indicates the twin has noticed something anomalous and is tracking it. The data flows in one direction.

## Character Archetypes

| Archetype | Visual character |
|---|---|
| **Functionary** | Standard Covenant kit, baseline. The composure is practiced, not forced — or it appears that way. |
| **Coordinator** | Same kit, different bearing. No visible rank distinction in gear — the authority is behavioral. |
| **Technical** | Clean lab/field hybrid. Equipment that implies precise work. The tools are clean. |
| **Perimeter** | The one visible acknowledgment that defense exists. Compact in silhouette, Covenant in material. The only Covenant gear with visible load-bearing architecture. |

## Relation to the Overlay

Covenant territory in the twin's overlay is anomalous. The data is present, accurate, and well-indexed — more than should be possible given that the twin's data layer reflects machine-layer activity, not human record-keeping.

The implication is that the Covenant is interacting with the twin in ways that other human factions are not.

Their Historical Markers, when present, are formatted differently from Warden documentation. The twin indexed them. The format is not Warden.

---

## Asset Notes

- The pristine state is the difficult constraint — every other faction has wear and damage. Covenant assets need a specific clean-but-not-new quality that avoids reading as sci-fi or corporate
- Underlayer violet appears only in overlay/VFX pass — no surface textures should carry it
- Minimal variation across Covenant characters is intentional and must be maintained
- The "wrong" quality must emerge from context, not from individual asset design — no single Covenant element should look strange in isolation
