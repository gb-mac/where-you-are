# Concept Brief — Iron Compact
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The ones who decided that order was worth the cost of enforcing it. They have supply lines, chain of command, and a clear idea of what the future looks like — and who is allowed to be part of it. They are not wrong that organization survives where improvisation fails. They are not right about what that justifies.

The emotional register is: **this is necessary.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Warmth axis:** Cold — closest to machine discipline of any human faction. Against the twin's overlay they almost disappear, which is either reassuring or unsettling depending on where you stand.

**Key hues:**
- Military slate `#4A5568`
- Cold olive `#556B2F`
- Matte black hardware
- Weathered dark tan `#8B7355` for worn field applications
- No warm accents — warmth is personal, the Compact is institutional

**Material language:** Maintained and uniform. The defining quality is consistency — the same gear, worn the same way. Repairs are invisible by design because a visible repair is a deviation from standard, and deviations are noted. Everything is functional, nothing is personal. The person wearing the gear is not the point.

**Silhouette:** Structured, load-bearing architecture legible at distance. Military silhouette — the geometry of organized force. Clear distinction between officer-tier and field-tier, visible in silhouette alone.

---

## Identity Markers

The Compact marking system is **institutional and standardized.** Applied by the organization, owned by the organization.

- Unit identifiers — alphanumeric, stenciled, consistent across all members of a unit
- Rank indicators — position on the left chest, not decorative, not personal
- Theater patches — geographic assignment marking, tracks where a unit has operated
- **No personal marks.** A Compact soldier who personalizes gear is a Compact soldier with a problem.

**The absence of personal expression is the marker.** In a world full of people who modified everything they owned to keep it alive, the Compact's uniformity is immediately readable as a choice — and a costly one.

## Character Archetypes

| Archetype | Visual character |
|---|---|
| **Enforcer** | Full standard kit, maintained to regulation. The gear is in better condition than most players' best equipment. |
| **Officer** | Same kit, different markers. The rank shows in the markers, not the gear quality — the Compact is not ostentatious. |
| **Logistician** | Compact base, supply-modified. More carrying capacity, less armor — they are behind the line, and it shows. |
| **Scout** | Compact base stripped down — noise reduction, signature reduction. The uniformity is still there but quieter. |

## Relation to the Overlay

The Compact maintains records. Their controlled territory in the twin's overlay is heavily annotated — census data, resource inventories, patrol routes, incident logs. The data layer over Compact territory is denser than almost anywhere else because they have been deliberately feeding information to the twin (or to systems the twin indexes).

Whether they know this is what they're doing is an open question.

Compact identity markers are tagged in the twin's facial and silhouette recognition layer. In Compact-controlled territory, the overlay knows who you are.

## Asset Notes

- Lowest material variation of any human faction — the uniformity must read immediately
- Three tiers of wear state: garrison (near-pristine), field (operational wear), extended-field (hard use, still maintained)
- Repair work is hidden — no visible patches, stitching is matched, hardware is replaced not repaired
- Officer/field distinction must be readable at 40m in the overlay — marker placement and scale must survive FSR
