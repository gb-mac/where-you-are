# Concept Brief — Fractured Tier Visual System
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Design Premise

Players must be able to read a Fractured unit's tier at a glance, at encounter range, under FSR. Tier determines threat profile, behavioral patterns, and the tactical information required before engagement. Getting the tier wrong is a combat design failure.

Tier identification works through three simultaneous signals:
1. **Silhouette mass** — how much has accumulated
2. **Coherent core legibility** — how much of the original chassis is still visible
3. **Accumulation coherence** — whether the additions look recent or established

---

## Tier 1 — Fresh

**Time range:** Hours to days post-Fracture
**Threat level:** Manageable. Patterns readable. This is the encounter that teaches players the Fractured design language.

**Visual:**
The original Coherent chassis is fully or mostly visible. The Fracture itself is legible — a specific damage event (blast scoring, EMP burn, Vael Displacement Zone proximity signature) that interrupted the twin connection. The chassis has not yet begun incorporating additions in any significant way. Whatever scavenging has occurred is limited and visible as recent and provisional — a piece of plating leaned against the chassis, not welded. A cable wrapped around a joint, not integrated.

**Movement:** Still moves like the Coherent type it was. Players who know Coherent movement patterns can read a Fresh Fractured completely. The difference from Coherent is behavioral — the purposeful task-focus is gone. The motion is territorial, agitated, circling. The same body language doing a different thing.

**Overlay:**
The void radius is small — a few meters. The twin has just lost contact. The edge of the void is slightly soft, like the twin is still trying to reconnect. Over time it will harden.

**Silhouette read at distance:** Same silhouette as Coherent equivalent. Distinction comes from behavioral read, not visual. Players learn the behavioral tells first.

---

## Tier 2 — Augmented

**Time range:** One to three weeks post-Fracture
**Threat level:** Increased. The additions have changed what it can do. Known Coherent patterns are present but overlaid with new capabilities.

**Visual:**
The Coherent chassis is still clearly visible — it is the primary structure. One to three significant additions are present: extra plating at vulnerable areas, a scavenged arm or tool repurposed as a weapon, a second sensor cluster relocated from a salvaged unit. The additions are attached, not integrated — the joins are visible and functional but not seamless. A player can identify what the addition was before it was incorporated.

**The zone signature begins here.** A week into its territory, the additions start reflecting what was available. An urban Augmented will look different from an industrial Augmented. The zone-typed material color palette becomes visible.

**Movement:** The additions don't always move the way they look like they should. A repurposed industrial arm attached to a delivery chassis has different weight distribution than the chassis was designed for. The movement is slightly wrong — the original motion patterns are still there but compensating for additions the design didn't include.

**Overlay:**
Void radius is established and hard-edged. The twin has accepted the loss of contact and stopped trying to reconnect. The void is a fact now, not a failure state.

**Silhouette read at distance:** Coherent base still recognizable. Additional mass at specific points — the additions are readable as additions. "Coherent with additions" is the silhouette read.

---

## Tier 3 — Composite

**Time range:** One to three months post-Fracture
**Threat level:** High. Treat as a new encounter type. Prior knowledge of the Coherent chassis is partial information.

**Visual:**
The original Coherent chassis is present but no longer dominant. The accumulation has covered or modified enough of it that the unit's current silhouette is primarily determined by what it has become, not what it was. The Coherent base is archaeologically present — visible as a structural core beneath the layers — but the outer form is the Composite's own.

The zone signature is fully legible here. A Composite from a harbor zone reads as a marine industrial accumulation. A Composite from an urban zone carries vehicle bodies, transit infrastructure, city signage. The accumulation is the biography.

**The zone material palette at maximum legibility:**

| Zone | Dominant accumulated materials | Color register |
|---|---|---|
| Rural / Agricultural | Combine harvester heads, auger coils, irrigation pipe sections, tractor cab | Oxidized agricultural yellow-green, machinery red `#8B2010`, hydraulic hose black |
| Industrial / Logistics | Crane cable spools, hydraulic piston clusters, conveyor belt loops, forklift forks | Rust orange `#8B4513`, industrial gray, chemical teal stain |
| Urban | Bus chassis sections, metro car panels, traffic gantry, parking barrier arms, vehicle bodies | Transit livery colors degraded, concrete gray, asphalt black, reflective signage |
| Coastal / Harbor | Anchor chain wrapped, crane boom sections, sonar array components, pressure hull plating | Salt-oxidized green-gray, nautical orange, bleached white, deep rust |
| Suburban / Residential | HVAC unit clusters, garage door rail systems, domestic appliance housings, home security arrays | Appliance white yellowed, suburban beige at machine scale, domestic color in wrong proportions |
| Remote / Wilderness | Comms tower sections, pipeline joints, mining rig arm components, weather station dishes | Industrial primer gray, tower steel, weathered red oxide |

**Movement:** Unpredictable reach and capability. The Composite may have incorporated construction hydraulics that give it a range its original design never had. A salvaged arm from a different machine type operates on different extension logic. The player encounters a unit whose capabilities they have to discover in the engagement.

**Overlay:**
Void radius is the largest for a mobile unit. The void edge is hard and well-established — this unit has been severed for long enough that the twin's records of it are historical.

**Silhouette read at distance:** The Coherent base is not the read anymore. The zone-typed accumulation is the silhouette. "Big, wrong, zone-typed mass" is the distance read. The tier is communicated by the ratio of accumulated mass to readable chassis.

## Tier 4 — Behemoth

**Time range:** Months without being stopped
**Threat level:** Area boss. World event if roaming.

**Visual:**
The original Coherent chassis is effectively a structural core — present, load-bearing, and rarely visible on the exterior. What the player sees is the Behemoth's accumulated form: multiple full-sized units incorporated into a single structure, zone materials at maximum density. The Behemoth has killed everything in its territory that could threaten it and used the remains to grow further.

**The scale is the primary horror.** Not the design, not special effects — the scale, against a reference the player knows.

**Stationary Behemoths:**
Too large to move effectively, they control a zone. The overlay shows the void radius as a significant area of black. Their territory is a place on the map. Players approach them as they approach a dungeon — it's there, it's known, you have to be ready.

**Roaming Behemoths:**
Slow but unstoppable. When a Behemoth walks, the AR overlay broadcasts a large moving contact to every player in a wide radius. The overlay shows the void moving — a black mass, hard-edged, crossing through territory.

**Zone-specific Behemoth design logic:**

*Rural Behemoth (primary design brief):*
The most massive. The scale contrast is the horror — a combine harvester chassis in a residential street. The harvesting heads repurposed as forward arms. The grain elevator conveyor coiled around the upper structure. The scale that made sense in an agricultural field now occupying a residential intersection, and everything about the environment communicates that this thing should not be here.

When a Rural Behemoth walks into an urban or suburban area: the scale comparison with doorways, parked cars, telephone poles, and building facades makes the size legible in a way open-field combat never could. The horror is not the machine. The horror is the machine against a backdrop where its scale makes no sense.

*Urban Behemoth:*
Multiple city bus chassis as the primary structure, metro car sections as armor plating, a traffic gantry reprocessed as a forward-facing structure. Faster than other Behemoths due to the vehicle-heavy accumulation — the movement systems incorporated are vehicle-scale. Uses the urban environment as a resource during the fight — vehicles in the environment can be incorporated on the fly if the fight runs long enough.

*Industrial Behemoth:*
Maximum structural mass. Crane arm reach extended over a large area. Chemical and heat-based area denial from industrial systems still cycling. The longest engagement radius of any Behemoth type. Players who fight it are playing a different game than players who fight a rural Behemoth — distance and exposure management versus scale shock.

*Coastal Behemoth:*
Amphibious territory. The fight may begin on land and continue into water. Marine diesel power. Anchor chain as a physical attack range multiplier. Underwater portions of its territory are inaccessible to most players without specific gear. A partially submerged Behemoth is not in a disadvantaged state — it is in its preferred state.

## Tier Legibility Rules

**At 80m:** Mass read only — Fresh/Augmented reads as machine-sized, Composite reads as large, Behemoth reads as structurally impossible in its environment.

**At 40m:** Zone palette visible, accumulation coherence readable — additions vs established accumulation.

**At 20m:** Coherent core legibility — how much of the original chassis is visible, what the additions are made of.

**FSR rule:** Tier must be readable at 40m under FSR Quality mode. The mass silhouette is the FSR-safe tier indicator — it must be bold enough to survive upscaling. Zone palette detail can degrade; mass silhouette cannot.
