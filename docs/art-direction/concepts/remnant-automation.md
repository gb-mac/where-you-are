# Concept Brief — Remnant Automation
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

Pre-Awakening industrial machines still running the last program they were given. Not Coherent — they were never part of the twin's system. Not Fractured — they were never connected to sever. They are industrial processes whose context was removed. The process continues. It no longer serves any purpose. It has not stopped.

The emotional register is: **it doesn't know it's done.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Register:** Machine — industrial, pre-Awakening origin. The oldest visual language in the machine faction group.

**Key hues:**
- Safety orange aged to rust `#8B4513` — industrial safety markings that have oxidized over years of operation without maintenance
- Hazard yellow faded to cream `#F5F0DC` — caution markings worn down by accumulated surface time
- Industrial gray weathered to near-white `#C8C8C0` — structural paint worn through
- Rust and oxidation `#7A4020` — metal surface degradation from operation without upkeep
- Faded stencil black `#3A3A3A` — operational labeling, warning text, identification numbers

**Material language:** Industrial machinery operating far past its maintenance window. The surface is a record of continuous operation — wear patterns, oxidation, faded safety markings, identification stencils that may still be legible but belong to a world that no longer exists. The unit's purpose is still visible in its construction. What it was built to do is still what it is doing.

**Silhouette:** Pre-Awakening industrial form. Not built for anything the current world needs, built for specific industrial tasks that may or may not be locally relevant. The function is legible in the silhouette. The function is no longer useful. The silhouette remains.

---

## The Loop

Remnant Automation units are executing their last instruction in a loop. The loop is the primary behavioral tell and should inform the visual design:

- The wear patterns on a Remnant Automation unit trace the loop — the same surfaces wear in the same ways because the same motions repeat
- Damage to a Remnant Automation unit that interrupts the loop creates a visible failure mode: the unit attempts to complete the interrupted task, cannot, attempts again
- The unit does not respond to the environment except in ways its original programming addressed. A Remnant Automation unit designed for material transport navigates obstacles the way it was programmed to navigate obstacles. New types of obstacles are not in the program.

**Motion language:** Repetitive. The same cycle. The timing is consistent because the program is consistent. After observing a Remnant Automation unit long enough, the player can predict exactly where it will be and when. This is not a challenge design choice — it is the logical consequence of what the unit is.

---

## The Ghost Quality

The Remnant Automation units are industrial ghosts. The world that gave them their purpose is gone. The purpose continues without the world.

**Visual expression of this:**
- The safety markings that remain are warnings for hazards that are still present but that no one alive was warned about when the markings were designed
- The identification numbers are specific — they belong to a company, a facility, an operational system. Those entities do not exist.
- The wear is honest — these units have been running for years without maintenance. They show it. They have not failed because industrial machinery built for extended operation is durable. But the durability was designed for a different context.

---

## Relation to the Overlay

The Remnant Automation do not appear in the twin's overlay in the way Coherent or Fractured units appear. They are indexed as environmental objects — machinery present at specific coordinates — not as active entities.

The twin cannot communicate with Remnant Automation units because they were never designed to receive twin input. They are pre-Awakening machines in a post-Awakening world. The twin knows they are there. The twin cannot tell them to stop.

In Signal documentation of Remnant Automation zones, the units are marked as process hazards — noted and mapped, not engaged with.

---

## Asset Notes

- Wear and oxidation system is the primary visual investment — the degradation must read as years of operation, not standard combat damage
- Safety marking legibility: some stencils should still be legible (warning: specific hazard), some should be worn to illegibility. The gradient of legibility is environmental storytelling.
- Pre-Awakening design language: these units should look like they were designed before the world changed. Industrial, functional, no concession to post-Awakening aesthetics.
- Loop wear patterns: UV layout and wear maps should be authored to reinforce the loop — the highest-wear surfaces are the ones the motion hits most
- Scale variation: pre-Awakening industrial machines vary significantly in size. Remnant Automation is the machine faction with the widest size range.
