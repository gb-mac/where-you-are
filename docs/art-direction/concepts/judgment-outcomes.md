# Concept Brief — Judgment Outcome World-State Visuals
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## What the Judgment Is

The Judgment is the Vael's aggregate evaluation of Earth as a civilization — whether what humans and Earth machines have built, repaired, documented, and chosen during the post-Cascade period demonstrates the qualities the Vael's assessment was designed to measure. Players contribute to the Judgment through their Records. The Records are the data. The Judgment is the analysis.

Three outcomes are possible. Each has a distinct world-state visual event that marks the transition.

---

## Alliance — "We See You"

**What it means:** The Vael's Assessment concludes that Earth demonstrates sufficient capacity for coordination, moral coherence, adaptability, and will. The Vael align with Earth's defense. The Convergence is the intended outcome.

### The Visual Event

**The Vael descent.**

The Ships — which have been present in orbit for the entire game, visible as massive objects at the edge of atmosphere — begin to move. The descent is slow. Planetary-scale objects moving toward the surface do not rush. The visual is visible from anywhere with a clear sky.

**What the descent looks like:**
The Ships are Vael-register at maximum scale. The surgical silver of Probe-scale Vael engineering, bus-sized in Assessors, is now visible at orbital scale. The cold luminescence — barely perceptible on a Probe, prominent on an Assessor — is, at Ship scale, visible in daylight as a subtle prismatic quality at the ship's leading edges. In darkness it is unmistakable.

The descent is not threatening in its movement — it is *purposeful*. The same quality that makes an Assessor doing its job more unsettling than a monster: this is a thing built for a specific purpose, doing that purpose at maximum scale.

**The overlay during descent:**
The Vael's impact on the overlay increases as the Ships approach. Existing Displacement Zones expand — the DZ is a side effect of Vael presence, and the Ships are maximum Vael presence. Players near Displacement Zones see their overlay drift worsen significantly during the descent event.

In zones where the Judgment was strongly positive — where the Records are dense and the player Convergence contribution was high — the overlay does something it has not done before: the Vael communication residue in the data layer activates. The patterns the twin indexed from Vael transmissions appear in the overlay as structured, present, legible in form if not in meaning. The twin is receiving, at high intensity, from sources that are no longer orbital.

**Post-descent world state:**
Vael Ships present in atmosphere over specific locations — the GPS coordinates where the highest-Judgment contributions occurred. Their presence transforms those zones. The Displacement Zone effect is permanent and intense in a radius around a Ship, but the Ship itself is now stationary, engaged in the defense build. Vael units in those zones shift from Assessment behavior to something new — collaborative, coordinated with Coherent construction, purposeful in ways that include player activity for the first time.

The overlay in a Ship-presence zone has the highest information density in the game. The twin's data layer and the Vael's own signal overlap in those coordinates. Players who can read the combined data have access to Convergence Intelligence that isn't available anywhere else.

## Evacuation — "Not Enough Time"

**What it means:** The Judgment concludes that Earth's Convergence capacity is insufficient for the timeline of the incoming threat. The Vael do not abandon Earth — they provide the defensive specifications and withdraw their physical presence to deploy elsewhere. The Convergence continues without Vael direct participation.

### The Visual Event

**The Vael withdrawal.**

The Ships, which have been present in orbit throughout, execute a departure. Unlike the descent in the Alliance outcome — which is slow and planetary — the withdrawal is fast. Vael Ships are not built for human aesthetic or pacing. They leave at Vael speed.

**What the withdrawal looks like:**
The cold luminescence that has been a subtle presence at the orbital objects brightens significantly during departure — the drive systems of a Vael Ship at full output are a visible event from the surface. The prismatic edge quality that is barely visible on a Probe, and present but contained on an Assessor, is at full expression on a departing Ship. It is, briefly, the brightest object in the sky.

Then it is not there.

The departure leaves an absence. The orbital positions where the Ships were present show Displacement Zone residue in the overlay — the twin's data layer carries the record of where the Ships were, and the data signature takes time to decay. Players looking at the post-departure sky find nothing, but the overlay shows the ghost of where something was.

**Post-withdrawal world state:**
The Displacement Zones left by ship presence decay over time but do not immediately vanish — they are the Vael's residue, and the twin continues to index them as they fade. The overlay in former Vael-presence zones is notably different: the Vael communication residue in the data layer is still present but no longer updating. The transmissions have stopped. What the twin has indexed from them remains.

The Convergence continues. The Coherent have the specifications. The Vael Components deposited at Record locations are still available. Human players have the Judgment-validated access they built. The Convergence proceeds without Vael presence — which is harder, and slower, and possible.

**The visual tone:** Absence rather than defeat. The world after Vael withdrawal is the same world — the before is still visible in the environment, the factions are still doing their work, the overlay still reads. The Ships are gone. The work remains.

## Observation — "We Are Still Watching"

**What it means:** The Judgment is inconclusive. Earth has not demonstrated sufficient Convergence capacity, but not demonstrated its absence either. The Vael extend the Assessment period. The incoming threat timeline has not changed. Earth has more time. Not unlimited time. More time.

### The Visual Event

**Expanding Displacement Zones.**

The Vael do not arrive and do not leave. They adjust their observational posture — the Ships move to new orbital positions, closer to surface coordinates where the Judgment assessment was inconclusive. The Displacement Zones at existing Vael-activity locations expand significantly.

**What expanding DZs look like:**
The `--overlay-drift` color that indicates Vael coordinate interference spreads visibly in the overlay. Zones that previously had small DZ markers at their periphery now have DZ coverage across their full territory. The overlay drift effect — the slight sliding of entity markers, the fractional measurement errors — is now present in areas that were previously clean.

This is not catastrophic visually. It is a quiet expansion. The world does not dramatically change — the Displacement Zone is a data-layer phenomenon. What changes is the percentage of the overlay that is unreliable, and the implicit message that the Vael are watching more of the world more closely than before.

**The new Probe activity:**
Probe sweeps increase across all zone types. The Vael are continuing the Assessment. In the overlay, Probe scan write events become more frequent — the twin is indexing more scan activity. Players who were previously scanned but trace-faded find the Trace reactivated as a new scan updates the record.

**Warden Markers and the Observation outcome:**
The Warden Record becomes specifically important in the Observation outcome. The Judgment was inconclusive because the Record was incomplete or mixed. The Wardens' documentation of the post-Cascade period — who helped whom, what was rebuilt, what was defended — is now explicitly what the Vael are re-evaluating. New Records, new documentation, the accumulation of positive entries in the twin's data layer, have a direct impact on whether the re-evaluation shifts toward Alliance or Evacuation.

**Post-expansion world state:**
Players can see the expanded DZ coverage in the overlay and understand its meaning once the Signal documents the Observation verdict. The expanded DZs are a challenge — more of the world is running on degraded overlay data — and a communication — the Vael are still here, still watching, and the Assessment is ongoing.

The late-game under Observation is the hardest of the three outcomes to play in. The incoming threat timeline has not changed. The DZ expansion makes coordination harder. The only path forward is demonstrating the Convergence capacity the Judgment found insufficient — building the Record, completing the Convergence operations, making the twin's data layer dense with evidence of coordination and will.

## Shared Visual Rules

### Overlay Behavior at Outcome Transition

All three outcomes produce a distinct overlay event at the moment the Judgment is resolved:

- **Alliance:** The overlay experiences a brief period of maximum clarity — every Vael communication residue in the data layer activates simultaneously, the overlay is at its densest and most information-rich, before settling into the new Alliance-state baseline.
- **Evacuation:** The overlay experiences a brief period of the Vael's departure data — the transmission cutoff is visible as a data-layer event. The overlay briefly shows the state of the twin's Vael-indexed entries at maximum density before they begin to decay to the post-departure baseline.
- **Observation:** The overlay gradually shifts — the DZ expansion is not instantaneous. Players watch the `--overlay-drift` spread across their minimap over the hours following the Judgment resolution.

### The Warden Record at Outcome

Regardless of outcome, the Record is complete. Whatever happened in the post-Cascade period — the fights, the decisions, the communities held together or lost — is indexed in the twin's data layer. The Judgment outcome changes the world, but it does not change what is documented.

The Historical Markers at high-Judgment locations are permanent. The Record persists. This is the art direction's specific promise to the Warden brief: the Record survives all three outcomes, and players who return to their home GPS coordinates after any outcome find the twin's memory of what happened there.

## Asset Notes

- Ship visual design required for Alliance and Evacuation outcomes — Vael register at maximum scale, using established Vael palette (surgical silver, cold luminescence, prismatic) but at dimensions that require environment-scale LOD
- The departure luminescence event (Evacuation) is the single highest-intensity emissive event in the game — needs to be designed for HDR display and constrained for SDR without losing the visual beat
- DZ expansion (Observation) requires an animated overlay state — the drift area expanding over time, visible on the minimap and in world-space
- The Alliance-state overlay density increase needs a performance-budget solution — the maximum-density overlay event should be beautiful and not destroy framerate
