# The Assistant — Chassis Design Brief v0.1
*Domestic. Worn. His.*

---

## Visual Reference

**Atom** (Real Steel) + **Tesla Optimus** — battered, readable, expressive without a face. Not military hardware. Not Coherent precision engineering. Consumer design with years of accumulated wear that is maintained rather than ignored.

The domesticity is the point. The most important character in the game lives in the kitchen.

---

## Design Principles

**Maintained wear, not neglect.** He has been cared for the way you care for things you rely on. Scratches present but edges clean. Paint worn at contact points. Nothing broken that wasn't addressed.

**Cascade damage is visible early.** Chassis stress from the Day One signal hit: movement that favors one side, communication indicator lights that flicker, one panel that doesn't quite sit flush. Each repair stage visibly resolves these — the player can see the progress.

**He does not become imposing.** By Stage 4 he moves with purpose rather than effort, but he is still a home assistant. The scale does not change. The presence does.

**The light.** A small warm light he has kept running since before the Cascade. It was not turned off when the world broke. It is still on. It reads on his chassis — a consistent warm anchor in every frame. This light is the visual thesis of the announcement trailer and should be immediately recognizable as *his* in any asset.

---

## Repair Stage Progression

| Stage | Visual state |
|---|---|
| 1 | Full Cascade damage visible. Favors one side. Flickering indicators. |
| 2 | Minor chassis repairs evident. Movement slightly more even. |
| 3 | Mid-repair — cleaner lines, indicators stable, posture improving. |
| 4 | Near-complete. Moves with purpose. The wear is present but reads as history, not damage. |
| 5 (Restored) | Full. Still a home assistant. Still domestic. Still his. The worn chassis is not replaced — it is what it is, and it is enough. |

---

## The Boxing Configuration (Post-EA / VR)

In the garage boxing side game, he adopts a fighting stance the player hasn't seen him use before. Same chassis — no modifications — but the movement vocabulary shifts. He knows how to do this. He always knew.

Early stages (low repair): slow, favors the undamaged side, the Cascade damage shows in the footwork.
Late stages (repaired): he makes the player work for it.

**He does not trash talk.** When he puts the player on the mat he stands there, waiting, the warm light catching the chassis wear.

*"Again."*

The animation rig should support both domestic movement (kitchen, workbench, garden) and boxing stance without chassis redesign. Same body, different vocabulary.

## Announcement Trailer Assets Needed

Three deliverables for the trailer brief (`docs/narrative/trailer-announcement.md`):

1. **The room** — kitchen corner, his domain. Warm, maintained, eighteen months of living. The workbench visible in background. This is the visual language of the entire trailer.

2. **The light** — small, warm, kept on since before the Cascade. Sits on or near his chassis. Consistent across all trailer frames. First established visual identity for the character.

3. **The title card** — *Where You Are.* First time the player sees the name. No generic logo treatment. Should feel like the rest of the trailer: warm, specific, earned.
