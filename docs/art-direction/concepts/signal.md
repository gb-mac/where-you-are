# Concept Brief — Signal
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

Researchers, archivists, infrastructure people, and the people those people trained after the world changed. Signal's primary orientation is *understanding what happened* — and the secondary orientation is using that understanding to keep people alive. They are not peaceful by choice. They are peaceful by preference, and the preference has survived because they are useful enough that others protect them.

The emotional register is: **we need to know.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Warmth axis:** Mid — functional, not sentimental. Warmer than the machines, cooler than Holdouts. The warmth comes from the equipment materials (copper, exposed circuitry, weathered plastics) not from the humans wearing them.

**Key hues:**
- Amber warning `#F5A623` — borrowed from industrial safety language, now carries Signal's frequency-marking system
- Exposed copper `#B87333` — visible wiring, heat-worn circuit surfaces
- Functional yellow-green `#8FA832` — field equipment that started life with safety markings
- Weathered off-white `#E8E0CC` — worn plastics, stripped-down base layers
- Equipment gray `#6A7080` — structural metal, housing, casing

**Material language:** Improvised tech infrastructure. Signal kit is built around equipment, not the other way around. The person is part of the rig — cables route through the outfit, antenna elements are worn because they need to be physically positioned, sensor packages are load-bearing because they are the primary tool. The gear accumulates purpose.

The academic origin is still visible. Layered clothing underneath the equipment, worn into shapes that suggest years of desk work and field work both. A Signal technician looks like someone who has been doing fieldwork for longer than their training anticipated.

**Silhouette:** Asymmetric by function. Signal operatives have equipment profiles — the silhouette is determined by what they're carrying and what they're running, not by a garment system. Two Signal members doing different jobs look related but not identical.

---

## Identity Markers

Signal markers are **technical labels applied for functional purposes** that have become identity markers by accretion.

- Frequency markers — `#F5A623` amber stencil on equipment, identifying operating band or network node
- Network identifiers — alphanumeric, look like maintenance labeling because they started as maintenance labeling
- Equipment specs and version marks — carried over from pre-Awakening research practice because the practice never stopped
- Personal academic/field notation — Signal members continue annotating their own gear as they would annotate research equipment. These annotations accumulate into a record of work done.

**There is no unified Signal insignia.** Signal can be identified by the functional annotation system, the equipment silhouette, and the amber frequency marks — but these are not a designed identity system. They are what happens when researchers apply their professional habits to their survival equipment.

## Character Archetypes

| Archetype | Visual character |
|---|---|
| **Data technician** | Maximum equipment density — antenna arrays, processing hardware, multiple displays. The person is infrastructure. |
| **Field researcher** | Lighter equipment profile, more mobility. Still asymmetric, still annotated, but optimized for movement. |
| **Communications** | Specialized antenna geometry, frequency equipment prominently positioned. The silhouette is dominated by the antenna profile. |
| **Archivist-runner** | Library on the body — data storage, documentation equipment. Protected carrying systems. The archivist has learned to move fast. |

## Relation to the Overlay

Signal interacts with the twin's overlay more deliberately and explicitly than any other human faction. Their technical practice involves reading and interpreting the twin's data layer as primary research material.

Signal infrastructure in the overlay reads as active data nodes — dense, structured annotation, clearly the work of people who understand what they're indexing. Where the Wardens document events, Signal documents the data layer itself.

Signal members can interpret overlay degradation states (Fractured voids, Vael coordinate drift, Corrupted wrong-data) with more precision than other factions. In the overlay, Signal-designated analysis points are marked with amber frequency-band indicators — the twin has indexed their annotation system as legitimate data input.

The twin trusts Signal data. Whether Signal is aware of this or is learning to use it is a Narrative question.

## Asset Notes

- Equipment asymmetry must be designed as a modular system — antennas, sensor packages, cable routing paths, display panels — applied combinatorially to produce the required visual variety
- Amber `#F5A623` frequency marks must be legible under FSR — minimum 2px stencil lines at 1080p output
- Cable routing is a design element, not an afterthought — cables should route with visible purpose between equipment nodes
- Weathering on Signal equipment reflects use, not neglect — dirty from field work, maintained for function
- Academic layer (underlayer clothing) should show through wherever the equipment doesn't cover — Signal people are wearing what they were wearing before they had to learn to run
