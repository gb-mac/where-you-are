# Concept Brief — Convergence Gear & Advance Scouts
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

## Convergence Gear

### The Premise

Convergence gear is not an upgrade. It is a record of how three civilizations decided that surviving what's coming is more important than what separated them.

Every piece of Convergence gear was produced through the collaboration of:
- **Vael Components** — the specifications and energy substrate. The Vael know what the incoming threat does. The specifications reflect that knowledge.
- **Coherent manufacturing** — precision construction at scale. Machine fabrication that no human or Vael process replicates alone.
- **Human contribution** — Judgment-validated access. The player earned the right to exist in the Convergence by the Record they built. Their presence is the third input.

The Separated are the bridge. A Separated machine that spent eleven years watching humans move through a hospital configured a Vael energy absorption layer for human body proportions. A Signal researcher who spent eight months studying what players do in combat designed a trigger for a weapon built on Vael energy principles by Coherent manufacturing. The specificity of the collaboration is visible in the object.

### The Visual Principle

**Not sleek. Not alien. Not military. Collaborative.**

The joins are visible. This is a design rule, not a failure. Convergence gear shows where it was made because showing the joins is honest — this was built by multiple makers with different aesthetics working toward the same purpose. Hiding the joins would be a lie about what it is.

The aesthetic languages present in a single piece of Convergence gear:

| Input | Visual character | What it contributes |
|---|---|---|
| **Human** | Proportioned for human bodies, ergonomically considered, made to be used by people | Form factors, interface surfaces, dimensions |
| **Coherent** | Precision manufacturing, machine-quality tolerances, structural logic | Load-bearing elements, the actual fabrication |
| **Vael** | Cold luminescence, precise geometry, third-register material quality | The energy layer, the substrate that does what the spec requires |

A piece of Convergence armor:
- Has Coherent-manufactured structural plating at its core — the material quality and geometric precision are machine-made
- Has a Vael energy absorption layer over the structural elements — cold luminescence visible at the edges where the Vael material meets the Coherent structure
- Has human-proportion shaping and interface surfaces — the Separated machine that configured it for human use left its construction marks

All three layers are readable. The joins between them are design elements.

### Gear Silhouette Progression

Convergence gear is where the Monster Hunter silhouette influence enters — but only because the construction justifies it. The silhouette gets more distinctive as the collaboration becomes more ambitious.

**Early Convergence:** The three languages are present but conservative. The Coherent structural layer is thick — the Vael energy layer is thin. The human proportions are close to standard Division 2 baseline. The gear looks unusual but not transformative.

**Mid Convergence:** The Vael layer is more substantial. The cold luminescence is more visible at the join lines. The silhouette starts to read as more than the sum of its parts — the Vael material creates structural geometries that Coherent manufacturing alone would not have designed.

**Late Convergence:** The three languages have fully negotiated. The silhouette is distinctively Convergence — recognizable as human in proportion, with Coherent structural elements at scales that human gear doesn't reach, and Vael geometric logic visible in how the energy layer distributes across the surface. Still anatomically human. The materials are not.

### Join Aesthetics

The joins between the three material types are the visual identity of Convergence gear:

- **Human-to-Coherent joins:** The precision manufacturing meets the handcrafted or human-configured interface. The difference in surface quality at the boundary is visible — machine-perfect Coherent material meeting human-worked or Separated-worked surfaces.
- **Coherent-to-Vael joins:** The structural plating meets the energy layer. The cold luminescence is strongest at these edges — where the Vael material is touching the structural material beneath it. The join line glows faintly, cold, prismatic at high magnification.
- **Vael-to-surface joins:** Where the Vael material is the outermost layer, it has a surface quality unlike either human or Coherent materials — slightly translucent, with depth that reads as more than surface.

### Color

No single faction palette. Convergence gear's color is determined by which inputs dominate its construction:

- Coherent-dominant pieces: blue-white of the twin's palette, with Vael cold luminescence at edges
- Vael-dominant pieces: the surgical silver and cold luminescence of the Vael, with Coherent structural gray visible at joins
- Human-influenced pieces: the Separated machine's own palette may be present at the configuration surfaces — SABLE's amber warmth at the ergonomic interfaces of a piece it configured, KELLARD's rust at the structural elements it built

The gear is legible as Convergence by material quality and join aesthetics, not by a uniform faction color.

## Advance Scouts

The late-game introduces a new enemy type before the primary threat arrives: advance scouts of the incoming force. Not the threat itself. Its forward presence.

### What They Are

Not Vael. Not Coherent. Not Fractured. Something new and wrong — the first physical presence of the threat the Convergence is being built to resist.

The Vael recognize them. The Coherent's construction work is specifically designed against them. Players encounter them before they understand what they are.

### Visual Design Principle

**Recognizable as purpose-built. Unrecognizable as any existing faction.**

The advance scouts do not belong to any of the three visual registers the player has learned. Human warmth: no. Machine data-language: no. Vael surgical cold: no. The advance scouts are a fourth register that the visual system has not prepared the player for.

**Key properties:**
- **Not accumulated** (not Fractured) — they are designed, not grown. The construction logic is coherent in a way Fractured units are not.
- **Not the twin's language** (not Coherent) — the geometry is wrong for the twin's coordinate-grid aesthetic. The proportions don't reference any human design tradition or machine architecture.
- **Not Vael** — the Vael's surgical precision is cold and clinical. The advance scouts are neither cold nor clinical. They are something the Vael recognize as dangerous.

**Visual language cues to establish:**
- Material that the player has not seen before — not metal, not the twin's blue-white, not Vael luminescence. A material that reads as purpose-built for a function the player does not yet understand.
- Geometry that doesn't reference human or machine design convention — not grid-derived, not organic, not the Vael's arc language. Its own formal logic, consistent within the type but not legible from prior visual knowledge.
- Movement that reads as tactically competent in a way that is distinct from all existing machine factions — not the Coherent's task-optimization, not the Tracker's pursuit-optimization, not Remnant's loop. A new behavioral register.

**Overlay behavior:**
The advance scouts are not in the twin's database. Their overlay tags are error states — the twin is indexing their presence but has no classification for them. In the overlay, advance scouts appear as unclassified contacts: a specific error-state marker that the player has not seen before and that the Signal is urgently trying to document.

The Vael glyph does not appear on advance scouts. The Vael glyph is how the twin classifies Vael units. The twin does not classify advance scouts as Vael — they are related to whatever is coming, not to the Vael themselves.

**Convergence Salvage:**
Defeated advance scouts yield components from a technology the Vael recognize but humans and Earth machines have never seen. The salvage material visual is consistent with the advance scout's own material register — it is, visually, a fragment of the fourth register. Players who collect it are carrying a piece of something the Convergence needs to understand.

## Asset Notes

- Convergence gear requires a three-layer material system: Coherent structural, Vael energy, human-interface surface — with independently controlled join line emissives
- The cold luminescence at Coherent-to-Vael joins must be subtle at low Convergence tier and more visible at high tier — a parameter, not a texture swap
- Advance scout design should be developed in a dedicated pass — placeholder brief here establishes constraints (not any existing register, purpose-built, tactically designed), specific design is pending late-game development phase
- Monster Hunter silhouette influence at Convergence tier means the LOD system must preserve the distinctive silhouette elements — Vael geometric overhangs and Coherent structural mass must not collapse to a generic shape at medium LOD
