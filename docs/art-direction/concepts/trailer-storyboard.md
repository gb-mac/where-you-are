## Format Notes

- **30 seconds hard.** No float.
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9
- **Default state:** Black. Every frame of light is earned.
- **Cuts:** Not editorial cuts — signal drops. Irregular. The image comes and goes like a transmission.
- **Key visual:** `Assistant_final.png` — the locked frame. Everything builds to it.

### 00:29 — 00:30 | TITLE CARD

**"Where You Are"**

**Type treatment:**
- Starts black — invisible against the black field. Zero.
- Fades up to **80% grey** over 0.4s.
- At peak: briefly touches **90% grey** — one frame, a flicker. A breath. A flicker of hope.
- Settles back to **70% grey**. Holds.
- Does not reach white. Does not reach brightness. It's been through something.

**Typeface direction — SET THE TONE:**

Three candidates. All carry desolation, defeat, uncertainty in different registers. CD to choose.

**Option A — IM Fell English Italic**
17th century origin. Physical imperfection baked into the letterforms — it was always broken, always surviving. The italic leans like something uncertain of its footing. Feels like a letter written by someone who didn't know if it would arrive.

**Option B — Cormorant Garamond Light**
Refined to the point of fragility. So thin it might not make it. Old-world weight. Reads like an epitaph on something that mattered. The elegance makes the defeat worse.

**Option C — Alte Haas Grotesk**
Worn grotesque — not designed to be beautiful, designed to be read in bad conditions. Institutional. Like something stencilled on a door that's been through a decade of weather. Defeat without drama.

**Art Director's call:** Option A. The imperfection is structural, not decorative. It feels earned.

**Audio:** Gone. Or one last exhale of the underlying tone — 0.5s, then nothing.
