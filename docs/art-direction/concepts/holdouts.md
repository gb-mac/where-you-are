# Concept Brief — Holdouts
**Version:** 0.1 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Who They Are

The people who stayed. Not because they had a plan — because they had somewhere to stay. Community-built, neighborhood-scale, held together by the specific shape of the people in it. Every Holdout outfit is a record of the last eighteen months.

The emotional register is: **we are still here.**

---

## Visual Identity

**Warmth axis:** Highest of all factions. The deliberate counterpoint to the twin's cold data layer.

**Key hues:**
- Worn ochre `#C4873A`
- Faded olive `#7A8C5E`
- Personal rust `#9B4E2E`
- Sun-bleached canvas `#D4C9A8`
- Repair thread — whatever color was available

**Material language:** Layered. Nothing is one thing. A jacket is a jacket over a flannel over a thermal over a patch where the elbow wore through. Gear shows its history of use and repair because discarding and replacing is not an option. Every visible repair is a decision that was made to keep something alive.

**Silhouette:** Soft edges, civilian base. Load-bearing additions are visible but not structured — a bag here, a strap there, arranged by need not training. No military architecture. The silhouette is unmistakably a person who was not a soldier.

---

## Identity Markers

Holdouts have no unified marking system. Identity is **personal and local.**

- Hand-stenciled neighborhood symbols — block identifiers, building numbers, street names
- Family marks — personal devices that mean nothing outside the immediate community
- Color-coding between allied groups (block-level agreements, not faction-wide standardization)
- Wear pattern itself is identity — a player who knows Holdouts can read approximate tenure and home zone from the state of the gear

**No two Holdouts look alike. This is a feature, not a production constraint to solve.**

---

## Character Archetypes

| Archetype | Visual character |
|---|---|
| **Block captain** | Most organized gear, still improvised — the organization comes from habit not supply. Visible local authority markers. |
| **Scavenger-specialist** | Maximum carry, equipment optimized for collection. Bags, containers, hooks. Worn into a specific shape by the work. |
| **Community medic** | Clean where clean matters — hands, instruments. Everything else is survival-worn. Identifiable by the specific care applied to one part of the kit. |
| **Perimeter runner** | Mobile, light, fast. Less gear than other Holdouts. What's there is chosen and trusted. |

---

## Gear Tier Progression (Player)

Holdout starting gear is the visual baseline for the early game. All other faction aesthetics are read against it.

| Tier | Visual state |
|---|---|
| **Spawn / default** | Whatever they had at home. Civilian clothes plus the first practical additions. |
| **Established Holdout** | A coherent system has emerged from the improvisation. Still personal, still warm, but there is intentionality now. |
| **Holdout-faction late gear** | The neighborhood has been here long enough to develop craft. Repairs are now good repairs. The patchwork has become a style. |

---

## Relation to the Overlay

The twin catalogs Holdout territory as inhabited, documented, claimed space. Holdout zones in the overlay read as dense with indexed human activity — location annotations, survival-relevant tags, historical markers from Warden documentation of Holdout-held events.

Holdout players are the most documented humans in the twin's database. The world remembers where they lived.

---

## Asset Notes

- Highest material variation of any faction — each outfit needs distinct silhouette reads
- Modular layering system required: base garment → midlayer → outer → load bearing additions → personal marks
- Repair and wear must be procedural or hand-authored at high density — clean textures break the brief
- Color palette is warm but not saturated — survivor gear fades
